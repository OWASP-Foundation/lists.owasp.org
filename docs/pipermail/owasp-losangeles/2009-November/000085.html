Hi Everyone,

Today I have the honor to announce that effective January 1st 2010, Tin Zaw
will be the new chapter leader for OWASP Los Angeles.  Tin is a very
accomplished individual with significant experience in the industry and
solid educational background. For the one who haven’t had the pleasure of
meeting Tin personally, his bio follows below.



A lot has been done during this year at OWASP LA. The mailing list grew over
168% and we were able to maintain 1 meeting per month with quality speakers
(A complete list of presentations is available on
http://www.owasp.org/index.php/Los_Angeles_Previous_Presentations). Talks
were free from sales pitch and we occasionally seeded other organizations
such as OWASP OC and ISSA with our speakers.  Although OWASP LA is not
currently the home of any OWASP project, OWASP projects were represented in
30-40% of topics discussed in our meetings.



To continue this work is not only Tin’s responsibility but ours as we all
benefit from having a strong chapter. I personally intend to work very close
with Tin during the first couple months and ask all of you to support his
efforts as well.



Please help me welcoming Tin as the new chapter leader!



Sincerely,

Cassio



About Tin:
Tin works a Security Architect at AT&T Interactive, publisher of
YellowPages.com and other websites, where he leads company wide web
application security efforts and identity management services. Tin is
involved with secure software development process, security requirements,
design and code reviews, and penetration testing. Before AT&T Interactive,
Tin worked as a software engineer for about 14 years at companies such as
Qualcomm, Inktomi, Symantec and MySpace. At Symantec, Tin was part of the
team that developed intrusion detection technologies that is now part of all
Norton security products. Tin holds Master’s degree in Computer Science from
University of Southern California and Bachelor’s in Computer Science from
Pittsburg State University. Tin is a Certified Information Systems Security
Professional (CISSP) and Certified Secure Software Lifecycle Professional
(CSSLP).
