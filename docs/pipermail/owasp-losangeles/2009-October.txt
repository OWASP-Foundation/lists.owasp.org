Course Description:
Of all the information security-related certifications available, no
certification is held by more information security professionals than the
Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) certification.
This course thoroughly covers the 10 Core Body of Knowledge (CBK) areas
represented within the examination:
 * Information Security and Risk Management
     o Security management
     o Using a top-down approach
     o Security administration
     o Policies, standards and procedures
     o Risk analysis
     o Risk management
     o Information labeling
     o Assigning roles and responsibilities
     o Security awareness and training
 * Access Control
     o Identification, authentication, authorization methods
     o Authentication methods, models and techniques
     o Models of access control
     o Access control models and methods
     o Accountability
     o Access control practices
     o Access control threats
 * Security Architecture and Design
     o Computer architecture basics
     o Operating system architecture basics
     o Process and memory management
     o Trusted computing base (TCB) and similar security mechanisms
     o CPU modes and rings of protection
     o Virtualization
     o Security models
     o The Rainbow series and Common Criteria
     o Open source and proprietary software
 * Physical and Environmental Security
     o Physical security risks, threats, and countermeasures
     o Planning for physical security
     o Physical security controls
     o Asset protection
     o Facility security
     o Electrical systems
     o Fire prevention, detection and suppression
     o Auditing physical access
 * Telecommunications and Network Security
     o OSI Model
     o The TCP/IP suite
     o Types of cabling
     o Data transmission types
     o LANs and WANs
     o Network devices
     o Network services and protocols
     o Types of networks
     o Telecommunication protocols and devices
     o Remote access
     o Malware
     o Fault tolerance
 * Cryptography
     o A look into the past
     o Cryptography concepts
     o Government involvement in cryptography
     o Types of cryptosystems
     o Public key infrastructures (PKIs)
     o Key management
     o Network consideration
     o Email security
     o Cryptoanalytic attacks
 * Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Planning
     o Threats
     o Business continuity
     o Disaster recovery
     o Business impact analysis
     o Backups
     o Off-site facilities and operations
 * Application Security
     o Types of software controls
     o Database security
     o The software development life cycle and security
     o Security in distributed systems
     o Web security
     o Change control
     o Executable content language security
 * Legal, Regulations, Compliance, Investigation and Ethical Considerations
     o Computer crime legislation
     o Intellectual property legislation
     o Conducting investigations
     o Incident response procedures
     o Ethics
 * Operations Security
     o Interfacing with operations
     o Configuration management
     o Media controls
     o Availability
     o Mainframe security
     o Email security
     o Facsimile security
     o Vulnerability analysis and penetration testing
     o Intrusion detection systems

Instructor Biography:
Dr. Eugene Schultz, CISM, CISSP, is the Chief Technology Officer at Emagined
Security, an information security consultancy based in San Carlos,
California. He is the author/co-author of five books, one on Unix security,
another on Internet security, a third on Windows NT/2000 security, a fourth
on incident response, and the latest on intrusion detection and prevention.
 He has also written over 120 published papers.  Dr. Schultz was the
Editor-in-Chief of Computers and Security from 2002 - 2007, is currently on
the editorial board for this journal, and is an associate editor of Network
Security. He is also a SANS instructor, member of the SANS NewsBites,
co-author of the 2005 and 2006 Certified Information Security Manager
preparation materials, and is on the technical advisory board of three
companies.

Dr. Schultz has previously managed an information security practice as well
as a national incident response team. He has also been professor of computer
science at several universities and is retired from the University of
California at Berkeley. He has received the NASA Technical Excellence Award,
the Department of Energy Excellence Award, the Information Systems Security
Association (ISSA) Professional Achievement and Honor Roll Awards, the ISACA
John Kuyers Best Speaker/Best Conference Contributor Award, the Vanguard
Conference Top Gun Award (for best presenter) twice, the Vanguard Chairman's
Award, and the National Information Systems Security Conference Best Paper
Award. Additionally, Gene has been elected to the ISSA Hall of Fame.

While at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory he founded and managed the
U.S. Department of Energy's Computer Incident Advisory Capability (CIAC). He
is also a co-founder of FIRST, the Forum of Incident Response and Security
Teams. He is currently a member of the accreditation board of the Institute
of Information Security Professionals (IISP).

Dr. Schultz has provided expert testimony before committees within the U.S.
Senate and House of Representatives on various security-related issues, and
has served as an expert witness in legal cases.
