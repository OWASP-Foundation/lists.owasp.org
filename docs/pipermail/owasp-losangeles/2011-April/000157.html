Green Dot is the leading provider of prepaid financial services in the
United States, and owns and operates the Green Dot Network, which is the
largest cash reloading network in the United States. Green Dot has over  90%
share of the US retail market and its products are currently sold in more
than 50,000 top tier retailers nationwide including Walmart, CVS/pharmacy,
Fred Meyer, Kmart, Kroger, Radio Shack, Ralphs, Rite Aid, Smiths and
Walgreens. Green Dot's products include Green Dot MasterCard® and Visa®
branded prepaid debit cards and the Green Dot MoneyPak®. The Green Dot
MoneyPak provides a convenient and efficient way for consumers to load cash
to prepaid cards, use cash to make purchases and pay bills, and add cash to

area. Green Dot and MoneyPak are registered trademarks of Green Dot
Corporation

* **POSITION OVERVIEW*: The Senior Systems Security Analyst is a key member
of the Information Security department at Green Dot Corporation. The Analyst
will primarily be responsible for leading the development, implementation
and maintenance of the Systems Security program across the infrastructure IT
groups. This person will ensure systems and solutions will adhere to
security best-practices, compliance and regulatory requirements (PCI-DSS,
GLBA, and SOX), performing vendor assessments to ensure they meet minimum
security requirements e.g. ISO 27001/2, SAS70, PCI compliance etc. The
Analyst will also work with Engineering and Operations on projects and
operational security requirements, performing Vulnerability Scans, Pen
Tests, Testing results analysis, education and feedback to system
owners/administrators to mitigate security gaps. The Analyst will act as
subject matter expert in all aspects of systems security.


*DUTIES/RESPONSIBILITIES/EXPECTATIONS*


   - Uncover design, implementation, and operational flaws that could be
   used to exploit IT resources using penetration testing and evaluation tools,
   manual testing methods, procedural and documentation review, and personnel
   interviews.
   - Research, recommend, evaluate and implement enterprise infrastructure
   security solutions that identify and/or protect Green Dot from potential
   threats and respond to security violations.
   - Use current information security technology disciplines and industry
   standards to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of
   information assets.
   - Perform vulnerability assessments/penetration testing and assist with
   remediation efforts.
   - Support and maintain existing security solutions and technologies
   - Recognizes and identifies potential areas where existing IT security
   policies and procedures require change, or where new ones need to be
   developed.
   - Identifies gaps in compliance with PCI-DSS, GLBA, and SOX.
   - Conduct investigations of security violations and breaches, provide
   reports and analyses, provide recommended solutions and participate in
   incident handling.
   - Security monitoring, analyzing security alerts (primarily from IDS,
   Firewall, Windows, Web, AntiVirus, etc) using a SIM tool and escalating them
   to local support personnel.


*QUALIFICATIONS**:*


   - *Minimum 5 years of security systems experience*
   - Security qualifications, CISSP and/or CCSP certification preferred
   - Strong understanding of network and system level threats and
   vulnerability exploitation
   - Experience with network intrusion detection and network analysis tools
   - Experience with Data Loss Prevention (DLP) and endpoint protection
   technologies
   - Strong knowledge of common network protocols and associated weaknesses
   - Strong working knowledge of Windows operating systems, Active
   Directory, and related GPOs as well as hardening standards and methodologies
   for each.
   - Strong working knowledge of server level security (email, database, web
   server, etc.)
   - Familiarity with cryptographic principals, and common encryption
   schemes such as symmetric/asymmetric encryption, hashing, SSL/TLS, IPSec,
   PGP, S/MIME, SSH, PKI.
   - Strong knowledge of centralized logging and security event management
   best practices,, event correlation, as well as experience with syslog data,
   Windows Event Log, and database, web server, dns, and dhcp log analysis.
   - Experience with common security vulnerability assessment and
   penetration tools such as Nessus and NMap.
   - Familiarity and experience with security standards and guidelines such
   as PCI-DSS (required), GLBA, and SOX.
   - Knowledge and experience with incident handling processes, methods and
   best practices
   - Knowledge of network, system, and application forensics as well as
   basic chain of custody, evidence preservation and digital investigative
   techniques
