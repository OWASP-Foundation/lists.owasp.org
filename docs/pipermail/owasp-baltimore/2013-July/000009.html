We will be holding a explanation of basic web pen testing followed by
a couple examples to try, at Unallocated Space in Baltimore. This will
be held on 11th July (the second Thursday instead of the first), to
take into account the 4th of July long weekend the week before.

We will be working through a couple of basic web application attacks
with examples.  We will finish by walking through the examples
step-by-step after participants have gotten the chance to try on their
own.  There will be additional examples for anyone that wishes to
attempt more challenges.

Google Maps to Location: http://goo.gl/maps/0nBxX

@Forgotten has participated in dozens of Information Security
Challenges as well as created/designed a few.  He is currently the
president of Unallocated Space.

Unallocated Space (UAS) is an active Community Tech Space that runs
entirely off the donations of its members and participants with the
goal of "Teach, Learn, Build." UAS hosts a broad range of classes,
activities and public events in a 1,600 sq ft space, a few minutes
south of BWI Airport.  Equipped with a wide range of skillsets, tools,
equipment, parts and projec ts, UAS welcomes DIY and tech enthusiasts
of all skill levels and interests.  UAS is open almost every evening
and would love to see you regardless if it's for events or any other
time we are open.  See http://www.unallocatedspace.org for upcoming
events or contactinfo at unallocatedspace.org with any questions you may
have
