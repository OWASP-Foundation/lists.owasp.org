Bruce,

I find the relative strengths of the stages of the Challenge to be a  
challenge to my students. Some of the later stages are far easier than  
the first stage. They often take > 15 minutes to discover the  
mechanism, and in most cases do not find it. The recent addition of  
adding the URL arguments made it even harder than before.

Although I agree that we must have a faulty access control mechanism  
in the challenge (it's my favorite!), but it should work up in hardness:

Stage 1: Easy, no more than a couple of minutes
Stage 2: Moderate: no more than five-ten minutes to work through
Stage 3: Moderately hard: no more than ten minutes
Stage 4: Hard - this is where the faulty authz mechanism should be

Currently, Stage 1 requires them to synthesize a new attack based upon  
first principles and to inspect their WebScarab traces carefully.

We haven't shown them anything like Stage 1 before. There's a lot of  
"what might be missing..." scenarios in the other sections. Can we  
either add a new AuthZ lesson to use this same principle, or can we re- 
order the stages so that they don't get frustrated and stop the  
exercise?

thanks
Andrew
