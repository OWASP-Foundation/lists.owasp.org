Andrew van der Stock wrote:
> Hi there,
> 
> Dave asked me to pass on a quick tidbit and elaborate upon a future 
> lesson. There's a new lesson coming soon to WebGoat near you, only 
> HttpOnly! Apparently it will tell you if your browser supports HttpOnly 
> properly (read and write) like IE 7, or somewhat (like IE 6 - which 
> protects reading but not writing), or not at all (like Firefox or Safari).
> 
> This made me think about the idea of a new lesson. But it will need a 
> bit of work for the pre-packaged WebGoat. This is setting the "secure" 
> option of a cookie. When you add this, the browser SHOULD:
> 
> Only allow the cookie to be set if it is transported over SSL or TLS
> Only allow the cookie to be read if it is transported over SSL or TLS
> 
> However, different browsers act differently. It would be nice to extend 
> WebGoat to show how this worked in real life and in the process, not 
> only extend the knowledge of the users of WebGoat, but extend our 
> understanding of how X brand browser works with the "secure" option.
> 
> This would require WebGoat to run up the lesson over HTTP and HTTPS. 
> Without costing an arm and a leg, this would require a self signed cert. 
> We could either ask the individual to create one, or we could do it for 
> them.
> 
> What are your thoughts?
> 
> thanks,
> Andrew
> 
> 

WebGoat as shipped has a self-signed cert already. It shouldn't be too 
difficult to write up a lesson as you describe.
