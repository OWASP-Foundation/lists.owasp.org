Next, I wanted to share some results with you.  I want to start a
blog, and after seeing so many security experts get owned by ZFO, I
wanted something that wasn't riddled with holes.  I started down this
path here:

http://www.subspacefield.org/~travis/static_blog_generators.html

The tradeoffs of using static HTML are discussed there, but it's
obviously more secure.  In addition to finding a boatload of static
HTML generators, I found myself having to learn the various markup
languages, template languages, and so on.  It was a surprisingly large
amount of work!  I'm thinking of breaking it out into seperate pages
and doing some feature comparison matrices.

Nevertheless, for those of us who like to share what we've learned, or
gathered, or distilled, being able to do so easily is important.  I
once heard Marilyn vos Savant (highest IQ in the world) say that if
you want a good vocabulary, keep your dictionary in an easy-to-reach
