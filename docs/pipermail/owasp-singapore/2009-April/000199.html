Frenky Tjioe wrote:
> Dear Onn Chee
>
> SGNIC has no legal basis to stop Silver Media from owning
> posbank.com.sg.  DBS may dispute the ownership, and Singapore Domain
> Name Dispute Resolution Policy (SDRP) will mediate it.
>
> Khushwant Singh of Straitstimes wrote about this issue few months ago,
> and some of my comments were quoted in his article.
>
> Similar to ACRA, SGNIC does not wish to slow down the name
> registration process. Everything is very much automated nowadays.
>
> With ACRA, you may register a company called "Ministry of Technology".
> I spoke to a senior official from ACRA last month during the Supreme
> Court openhouse, she confirmed it. The only reserved name according to
> the current law is "Temasek". I was surprised when I was told that a
> company called "Singapore Arms" is actually selling automotive parts.
>
> SGNIC does prevent registration of certain names such as "Singapore",
> "Singapura", "Singapore Government", "President", but certainly not
> POSBANK.
>
> Regards,
> Frenky Tjioe
