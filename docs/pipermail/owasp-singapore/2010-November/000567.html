*_Kaspersky Lab Asia -  Asia Pacific Cup Conference for Young
Professionals_*

*_Security for the Next Generation_*

Dear Sirs,

 

Kaspersky Lab Asia is seeking to find young talented students in
Singapore to be a part of their Asia Pacific Cup Conference. 

 

Kaspersky Lab is a leading developer of secure content and threat
management solutions who recognize the importance of providing a
platform for the next generation to share their knowledge and develop
new ideas to improve the level of security in information technology. 

 

We would like to invite your students to apply for a chance to be a part
of this dynamic new initiative.

 

To qualify for the Asia Cup Conference, students have to register and
submit a research paper of between 7,500 to 8,000 words on a set topic
on IT Security by 30^th November 2010.  The top 3 participants with the
best papers from Singapore will be awarded CASH of between US$500 to
US$1,000 and be invited to attend the Asia Pacific Conference to be held
at the University Technology of MARA (Malaysia) along with their peers
from across the region in 2011.

In addition, the top 3 entries will qualify to compete at Regional level
for more awards and prizes.  The top 3 papers from Asia Pacific will
then move on to the International level and win a chance to be showcased
at the International Conference.

 

The aim of the conference is to allow students to actively participate
in the development of science and interact with other talented young
security specialists from around the world.

This is a fantastic opportunity for students to gain valuable knowledge,
showcase and be recognized for their talent as well as make their own
contribution to the global scientific community.

Students who are chosen to participate will be given the chance to meet
industry experts, scientists and researchers in a collaborative
environment to present ideas and discuss issues relating to Cybercrime. 
This will be their chance to forge and maintain valuable professional
ties that may lead to exciting career opportunities.

 

Local students are set to benefit enormously from this experience.

 

We hope that you will encourage your students to seize this opportunity
to represent Singapore and showcase their talent to the world by either
forwarding the information to them or by telling them about this
initiative.

 

Below are the details of how to apply for the conference, if you have
any further enquiries or require additional information please do not
hesitate to contact me.

 

 

Kind regards

 

 

Lim Woan Ying

*_How to Apply:_*

*_ _*

*_ _*

*_Phase I – County Cup (Singapore)_*

*_ _*

1.       _Registration and More Information_

Students who wish to participate MUST register by *_November 30^th 2010.

_*studconf at kaspersky-asia.com <mailto:studconf at kaspersky-asia.com>

 

 

2.       _Submission_

Students must prepare and send their research submission to the
organising committee by *_30^th November 2010._*

 

3.       _Who Should Participate_

Bachelor and Master Students studying in Singapore

 

4.       _Topics:_

Research should be based on one of the following topics:

_
Technical_

                         i.          Trends in Anti-Spam Development
Techniques, Methods in the Spam Arms Race and New Innovation

                        ii.          Danger of an Increasingly Networked
World

                      iii.          In the Cloud-Security

                      iv.          Future Technologies for Detecting and
Combating Malware (e.g artificial intelligence, fuzzy systems,p2p-networks)

_Social, Economic and Legal topics:_

                  v.          Emerging Threats (e.g social network
security, embedded systems security, mobile security, online banking
security)

                 vi.          Challenges and Opportunities for
IT-Security Companies Within the Next 10 Years

               vii.          Impact of technologies on Data Protection,
Copy and Intellectual Property Rights and Jurisdiction

              viii.          Education in IT Security- Trends and Questions

The research submissions will be evaluated by a committee.

Results of the Country Cup (Singapore) will be announced *_December
30^th 2010._*

The best three submissions for Singapore will qualify to compete in the
Regional Cup for Asia Pacific.

 

 

5.       The paper should include:

 

·      A clear introduction to the topic and aims of the paper

·      A section presenting background/literature review

·      One or more section presenting the students main contribution
(i.e. approach + and any associated results.

·      A clear conclusion

·      References in Harvard standard format.

 

6.       _Evaluation Criteria:_

 

·      Scientific + Methodological level of the work in competitive projects

·      The social importance + relevance of the work

·      The completeness of the contents

·      The objectivity of the info and analytical materials

·      The practical value

·      The innovation value

 

*_Phase II - Regional Cup_*

Results of the Regional Cup will be announced *_January 15^th 2011._*

The best three submissions per region will receive awards and prizes
from Kaspersky Lab and the authors will be invited to represent the
region in the Security for the Next Generation Conference hosted at the
University Technology of MARA (Malaysia) on February 2011.

The overall best three papers for the region will enter and compete in
the International Cup 2011

 

*_ _*

*_Phase III- International Cup_*

Students will have the opportunity to compete against other young
talented scientists from all other regions.

 

The conference will take place in April 2011 in Munich/Zurich (TBC.)

 

*For further information and submission rules and regulations please
visit **http://www.kaspersky.com/events**or email:
studcon at kaspersky-asia.com <mailto:studcon at kaspersky-asia.com>.*
