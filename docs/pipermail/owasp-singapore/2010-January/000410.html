Hi Ruel,

I think I can write a 10 page paper to answer you :-)

Short answers:
- Probably between 30 & 40% of attacks are from insiders. If there is
not a reliable audit trail, then you won't catch him or her. And if a
company does catch him? In Singapore, there's a good chance that a
company will not prosecute in order to save face.

- If an employee has access to an "internal web application" but is
using it from home and possibly from a home computer (not a
company-issued laptop), then there are browser cross-domain issues
which essentially can make the internal web application an external
one and subject to the usual types of attacks (CSRF, XSS, etc).

- The web applications might be internal; the data that is consumed
and displayed by the apps are from a database. What are the sources
that are entering data into that database? From other web apps? From
PDAs in the field? From a 3rd party call center in India or the
Philippines? No matter... if your "internal" web apps are using
untrusted data, then it has to sanitize output or you could be
infecting your "internal" users.

I could go on and on but I hope you get my point.
