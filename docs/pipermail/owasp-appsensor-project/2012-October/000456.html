I agree with Dennis' sentiments. Michael has done a great job coming up
with the AppSensor concept and leading the project. AppSensor will miss his
skills, but giving up being leader, doesn't necessarily mean you stop
participating.

Just in case there is any more fishing going on, I do not have time or
desire to be a leader of this project, and I think there are better people
for that. I want to continue as a contributor though (and intend to do some
more work on the v2 book soon!).

>> AppSensor Team & OWASP Leaders,

>> Just over 4 years ago I created the AppSensor project as part of OWASP's

>> core, developed into a book, built a sample implementation, multiple
>> guides, been the topic for academic research projects and even participated
>> in the Google summer of code.  We've discussed the project around the world
>> at conferences, with corporations with governments and more.  We've even
>> been published in the Journal of Defense Crosstalk magazine (
>> http://www.crosstalkonline.org/issues/septoct-2011.html) and are
>> featured on the Department of Homeland Security's website for building
>> resilient software (

>>
>> It's been a great ride to say the least.  Many thanks to all of our
>> contributors with a special shout out to Colin Watson, John Melton and
>> Dennis Groves.
>>
>> Letting go of a project like this is always tough. But it's what is best
>> for the long term success of the AppSensor project.  As many project
>> creators know, you don't want to walk away entirely and disappear from the
>> project.  I propose the idea of a "Project Founder" status for OWASP
>> projects.  I'll continue to be involved, volunteer and advise, but the
>> direction forward will be built and led by our next AppSensor Project
>> Leader.
>>

>> leader. This individual will shepherd the project, be the go-to for key
>> items and will build and drive the project's roadmap.  They won't be alone
>> of course - our whole AppSensor team is still here of course! But the
