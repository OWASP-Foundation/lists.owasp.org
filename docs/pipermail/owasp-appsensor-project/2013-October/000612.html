Yes without the staff team this would not have happened at all.

I found the comments interesting, especially

   "There was general consensus among reviewers that this [HOST] project
    is a small investment for a valuable part of the Appsensor Project, ..."

and how being associated with OWASP and being a voluntary effort assisted
our bid:

  "The reviewers also noted the importance of OWASP and the fact that
   it is volunteer-driven and therefore deserves support to remain viable."

I guess being vendor neutral also helps.  As a reminder about what we
applied for, the discussion thread is here:


http://lists.owasp.org/pipermail/owasp-appsensor-project/2013-August/thread.html

and the submission here:


https://docs.google.com/a/owasp.org/document/d/1cFbmOLqEQQG8eXPrMTlU6JUivgRIacUeL7D599bZm_E/edit

The submission was at fairly short notice and we bid for support for
something we can deliver very soon, in this case book and flyer
professional design

   "...including useful documentation of a high quality."

But in our August discussions we also thought perhaps applying for other
software development funding in a future round if this went well and the
opportunity still existed. And the discussion homed in on a
reporting/dashboard tool.

    "They also believe the software components and method will actually be
usable,
     and define sound practices for handling security issues at the
applications level. "

The combination of documentation and code makes AppSensor highly attractive.

I think many AppSensor contributors will be at AppSec USA, so it would be a
good opportunity to discuss this funding and ideas for 2014.

Meanwhile, we now need to get the book text finished :-)

Colin
