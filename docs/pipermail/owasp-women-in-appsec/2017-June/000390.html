During our call yesterday, there was a question around activities happening
at the sub-committee level and related to AppSec USA 2017

I can't speak to any sub-committee work, as I haven't been a part of any,
but I can speak to the work that I've done on behalf of WIA that may not
have been entirely visible:

1. Crafted the first draft of the WIA purpose and scope.  This was
discussed and edited during one of our calls and the final version is on
the WIA wiki.

2. Worked with Tiffany to get WIA officially recognized as an OWASP
Committee.

3. Created the membership list and I have been maintaining it until a
Secretary can take over the responsibility.  I shared it at a previous
meeting and there's now a link to it on the WIA wiki.

4. Gathering nominations for committee roles.  And I'll be running the
election next week.

5. Edited the wiki to bring it current.  You can see my changes by viewing
the wiki history.

6. Spoke with Emily about ideas for WIA at AppSec USA 2017, which I'll
explain below.

7. Worked with Emily and OWASP Global to have my employer sponsor the WIA
luncheon at AppSec USA 2017.

8. Setup doodle polls and scheduled meetings earlier in the year.

9. Probably other actions that I no longer remember.  I've pitched in to
help here and there when time allowed.

Following up with #6 above, with regard to AppSec USA 2017.  At AppSec USA
2016 (last year), Emily and I spent a few hours at the end of the
conference in person and debriefed what worked, what could be improved in
2016, and how we could "go big" at 2017, e.g. plan a
conference-in-a-conference, offer two days of free training, offer more
mentoring, etc.

I was prepared to start recruiting colleagues and friends to come speak at
AppSec USA 2017 last November.  But we immediately had a problem - we
needed the AppSec USA organizing committee to confirm that we could have a
room for the duration of the conference.  We couldn't start recruiting
speakers and planning an event if there wasn't any space for us.

To this day, the AppSec USA 2017 organizing committee has not confirmed we
have any space at AppSec USA, or at least not to me, and I've asked a bunch
of times.  I believe we space for the WIA luncheon, but that came
second-hand when setting up the sponsorship.

If we have space at AppSec USA 2017, then depending on how large the space
is and how long we have it, we should quickly decide what we want to do.
We can discuss options on the next WIA call on July 11.  It's currently the
first agenda item.  Maybe Tiffany can find out if we have space?

I hope that clarifies the question asked on the last WIA call.


- Bil
