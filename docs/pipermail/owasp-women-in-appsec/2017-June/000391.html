Hi Bil,

Wow! Yes, you have been one of our most active members. Many of the
activities that you list were also supported by other committee members,
and some, like the spreadsheet were just good things that you did on your
own. Thank you for sharing the long list of things with the committee. We
can go over them, as well as other things that others may have done, too,
in our next meeting. I also reached out to AppSecUSA and heard back from
their main contact organizer who said that they are having a hard time
getting things moving due to lack of local volunteer support. I suggested
that we might be able to find a few dedicated volunteers who could help
organize virtually and offered to have a phone call soon to discuss. If
you, or other WIA members are interested in attending this call, let me
know. I will post the info to the list.

I like your idea of inviting the AppSecUSA organizers to our next WIA
meeting, and I believe that would help open communications. There is an
#AppSecUSA Slack channel. You can see most of the discussion there. Let's
continue to post our activities to the list so that other WIAns can also
support efforts. Thank you and best wishes.

For the space ideas, in the past we had a room for WIAns to drop in during
the conference and spontaneously convene. We also had the mentor reception
and mentor luncheon that were very successful. For other programming, we
also had a diversity panel with men and women working on issues related to
diversity in AppSec. Perhaps we could have a wiki-a-thon to train some WIA
volunteers how to keep up the WIA wiki, and talk about ways to engage more
WIA led activities. At one time there was discussion about creating a
global WIA meetup. I'm in favor of that. Also local chapter WIA events, as
well as regional WIA events throughout the year, not just at AppSecUSA and
AppSec.EU

Sincerely,
Bev
