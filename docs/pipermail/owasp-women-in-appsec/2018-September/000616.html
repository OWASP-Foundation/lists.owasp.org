Hi all,

I don't have a whole lot more to add to this except to reiterate what Bil
has already said and state that the board didn't over step the mark - they
were simply implementing a recommendation made on the back of a report from
(this) compliance committee. So really your complaint is with our
recommendation?

I think the line you quote below Zoe ("Members of the OWASP Board of
Directors are allowed to become committee members, but participate as
normal committee members with no special powers either expressed or
implied. While Board member participation in committees is encouraged,
Board members must refrain from taking an active leadership role for the
committee.") isn't really intended for this situation - it is just
highlighting whether or not the board can be involved in a committee, not
what they as BoDs can do as part of there roles within the foundation.

With regards Bil, currently he is simply running for a BoD seat and is
still a member of the compliance committee so it is entirely appropriate
for him to respond to your complaint. As Bil mentioned, it was the middle
of the night for me as this discussion was taking place so I am only
getting the chance to respond now.

Katherine, thanks you for clarifying your stance. So far we have only heard
from yourself and Zoe on this issue. If anyone else in WIA would like to
respond to this thread to give their thoughts please do. Or you can respond
just to compliance at owasp.org if you would like to do so in confidence.

Kind regards,
Fiona

Fiona Collins,
OWASP Cork Chapter Lead
