Thank you all for you comments.  Here is the position of the Compliance
Committee:

1. Regarding the complaint by Zoe against the Board: The Compliance
Committee does not view the actions take by the Board as violating the
policies of OWASP.  If the Board meant to disempower themselves from
committees chartered under the Committees 2.0 policy, then the 2.0 policy
should be updated to reflect this.  If you are interested in having this
change made, propose it to the Board and have them vote to adopt it.  I
would suggest outside counsel review this change though, because the
members of the Board have personal liability in some circumstances, and if
a 2.0 Committee takes action that exposes OWASP to liability, it could be
the members of the Board are liable, yet have no power to address the
liability.  I personally would not serve on the Board if such a change were
adopted.

2. Regarding the complaint by Bev about the Compliance Committee
investigation and findings: The Compliance Committee welcomes feedback on
how we can improve our investigation model for future engagements.
However, once we hand off the case to the Board, it is closed on our side
unless the Board returns it to us.

3. Regarding the complaint by Bev about the Board: The Compliance Committee
views the actions taken by the Board as within their power and scope.  If
Bev wishes to appeal their decision, she must petition the Board.  Perhaps
a more formal appeal process should be created?  If so, please email the
Governance list with the suggestion.

4. This case, from the Compliance Committee's perspective, is closed - it
was handed off to the Board.  This means if you disagree with the outcome,
the investigation, the process, the rulings, etc, then you must take it up
with the Board.  The Compliance Committee has investigative power and the
ability to issue reports and recommendations to the Board, but we do not
have the power to implement policy changes or reverse decisions, that power
rests solely with the Board.


Best regards,

- Bil
