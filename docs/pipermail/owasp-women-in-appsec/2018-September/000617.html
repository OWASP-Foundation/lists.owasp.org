>>> Addressing your concern that the Board has taken action you perceive as infringing on the rights of the WIA committee, keep in mind they action they took was in response to the Whistleblower policy (https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Governance/Whistleblower_Policy), which dictates how complaints within the Foundation are handled.  There isn't a separate complaint process for WIA, so the Board was acting within their stated complaint policy.  Again, if you don't like this policy, it can be changed, but it does require the Board to vote to change it.
>>> 
>>> 2. You are correct, I am running for the BoD, as is Richard.  Note that Richard stepped down from the Compliance Committee and isn't included on the Compliance Committee email distribution - only Fiona and I are members currently.  You're welcome to add him to the thread anyhow if you want his opinion.  Fiona is on the thread, and likely hasn't chimed in due to time zone differences (she's in Ireland currently).
>>> 
>>> 3. Anyone on this thread is welcome to participate in this discussion.  I'm personally keen to understand why WIA would fight so hard to reinstate a member that was investigated for actions taken while volunteering for WIA and was referred to the BoD for discipline.
>>> 
>>> 4. I'll add another thought, let's change the scenario a bit.  Let's say you belong to the Awesome Committee and someone on the committee did something in violation of the Code of Ethics, or other OWASP Policies.  Is it your expectation that you must have your complaint handled by the Awesome Committee, who might have a conflict of interest because the leadership of the committee have a relationship with the person you're making a complaint against?  And since there isn't any written policy or procedure for handing the complaint, they could outright dismiss it, leaving you with no recourse but to leave the Awesome Committee.  And if you took your complaint to the Compliance Committee, the Awesome Committee would complain that their autonomy is being violated, making them unaccountable to OWASP Foundation.  I don't see how how your ask would fairly serve the members of OWASP when applied globally, not to just this one case.  But again, you could carry this forward and have the Board vote to enact it.
