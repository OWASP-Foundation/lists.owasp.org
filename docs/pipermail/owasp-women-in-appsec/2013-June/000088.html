Hello Bev,

Thank you for your comments. Please see my responses inline in blue below.


1. Do you think that we should do some kind of survey of the OWASP
organization members to find out ways that we can improve the scholarship
program? For example, perhaps we need to create programs for Veterans
(women and men) and also a program for Men in Application Security, as
well?  The costs for attending AppSec are very high, and application
security training, in general, is much higher than many other fields. There
are likely many underserved, and qualified Veterans (men and women), and
men who have high potential, as well, whom we could try to help with
scholarships, etc. Wouldn't it be nice if we could provide scholarships for
anyone interested in the programs by offering additional scholarship
programs? I'm interested in surveying interest, and having the
conversation, in order to meet the needs of all of our members, and
potential members. Your thoughts?

I think this is an excellent idea; however, I don't believe we will be able
to do this for the 2013 AppSec USA. It is certainly something we should do
for 2014. At this point, we really need to start raising the funding needed
for our Women in AppSec Program. We need to focus on getting this sorted as
soon as possible, as we have not raised any funding as of yet. Bev, were
you able to get in touch with Dana? Let us know.

2. Do you know of any others, especially OWASP men who might be interested
in serving on our program committee?  I asked friends to review our web
page, and one commented that we only have women serving on the current
committee, and that it would be nice if we had supportive OWASP men on the
committee, too. What do you think?

That would be great. They are all welcome. Just send us their e-mails and
we will add them to the list. We need more people looking for sponsorship
for the program, and could use more hands.

>>> *OWASP Women in Application Security (AppSec) Program*
>>>
>>> *Seeking Sponsorship*
>>>
>>> The OWASP Foundation, in recognition of value to both organizations and
>>> society, is working to support and enhance programs that increase the
>>> participation of women in the field of application security.  As part
>>> of this effort, OWASP is currently seeking funds to sponsor women from
>>> North America to attend  OWASP AppSec USA to be held in New York City in
>>> November of 2013.
>>>
>>> *Objectives*
>>>
>>> OWASP’s current program objective is to encourage female students at
>>> both the undergraduate and graduate levels, instructors, and professional
>>> workingwomen who are dedicated to a career in information security and/or
>>> application development, to expand their skills and pursue application
>>> security. Attendance for two women to the OWASP NYC AppSec USA 2013
>>> conference, and at least one of the in-depth training sessions, will be the
>>> award for this year's winners. The Women in AppSec Program was
>>> successfully launched at AppSec USA in 2011 this year's event hopes to
>>> build on that trend and further engage women in the community.
>>>
>>> *Sponsorship Needs*
>>>
>>> The OWASP Women in AppSec Committee is currently leading the effort to
>>> seek sponsors for this program.  Our goals are to raise funds to sponsor at
>>> least two attendees:
>>>
>>> - $3,000 for recipient #1 to cover conference fee, training fee, travel
>>> and accommodation.
>>>
>>> -  $3,000 for recipient #2 to cover conference fee, training fee, travel
>>> and accommodation.
>>>
>>> *Sponsor Opportunities: What do our sponsors receive? *
>>>
>>> All OWASP Women in AppSec sponsors will receive the following benefits
>>> for contributing funds to this program:
>>>
>>> - Sponsors mention in the AppSec USA Program
>>>
>>> - Logo, company name, and link to company website will be added to the
>>> AppSec USA website in the Sponsors Page<http://appsecusa.org/2013/sponsors/>
>>> .
>>>
>>> *Past Winners*
>>>
>>> Being fortunate enough to receive the Women in AppSec grant is a unique
>>> and valuable experience. It is a great opportunity for women to have a
>>> chance to bolster their skills and dive deep into the world of application
>>> security. I found that attending the conference was not only a great way to
>>> experience what the OWASP community has to offer, but it also gives
>>> students a chance to network with a great group of people who are
>>> passionate about their field and willing to share a wealth of information.
>>> ~ Tara Wilson, 2011 Winner
