Hi Wendy,

Thank you for preparing this. It is a great panel concept. Unfortunately,
so far, OWASP Global has refused to allow us to have a panel for this
year's AppSecUSA, however, I have asked them to reconsider, but so far, the
answer is still "no". I'm very disappointed in this because Bil and I both
reached out to the AppSecUSA Committee months ago to request WIA
programming be included in the AppSecUSA event. Only recently did we
receive a response from the Event Organizer informing us that programming
was already decided and WIA not included. They agreed that we could plan
programming and submit our request by the July 10th deadline. We were also
informed that there are women speakers. I requested their information so
that we could make them aware of WIA activites and invite them, and other
speakers, to participate if interested. I have so far received no response.
Of course, I protested this "selective" and what I consider "dismissive"
treatment by OWASP Global to the WIA committee and requested that there be
a change in the current process as I do not believe that it alligns well
with the OWASP Board's strategy for the OWASP Foundation. In any case, at
this stage, instead of putting any more efforts into a panel at this event,
perhaps you might be willing to focus on inviting the current speakers on
the current program to participate in the WIA activities? What are your
thoughts? Also, we need someone who is willing to take responsibility for
the WIA seat at the AppSecUSA registration desk. Would you be willing to
take responsibility for that? We can work on having a WIA panel at
AppSecUSA and EU 2018, and submit our sincere disappointment about the way
that things were handled this year to the OWASP BoD. There is also a
Leaders Review Group that anyone is welcome to join where we review things
like this. I will add this item to our list for discussion among other
leaders at the leaders reviews meetings. Thank you and best wishes.

Sincerely,
Bev
