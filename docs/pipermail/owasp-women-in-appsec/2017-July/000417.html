>> 2017-07-06 10:49 GMT-05:00 Ashley A <ashley.a.holtz at gmail.com>:
>>> Great suggestions, Bev! I've done a lot of women in security panels and wanted to expand on your panel idea. Perhaps we could find companies that have decent representation (more than the industry average for security of 11% women or more than the industry average for IT of 20%) or are making good improvements and ask them targeted questions on how they did it. This would make a panel or talk more focused on actionable steps, instead of just discussing the problem we know exists already. I'd be happy to help find these companies or help with a panel/talk like this. 
>>> 
>>> Another idea is inviting 3-5 local colleges to have CS/CE/EE professors nominate a diverse student to give a 10/15-minute "first conference talk" where they discuss a project, security trend, or research initiative. We could provide mentors from our group to make sure they're successful, and it's a great resume builder.
