Hello everyone,

Bev, thank you for taking the lead on this! 

I love all the ideas.  I have two more to add (below).  However, can we actually do all of them?  Do we have a budget for this (and if so, do we want to spend such a large percentage on one event)?  Do we have enough resources (hours and people) to do all of these activities?  If we have to choose, how do we choose?  Is there precedent for any of these questions?  I LOVE AppSec, but I don’t want that to be the only place we spend our donation dollars and our volunteer hours.  Women who are attending AppSec are already interested in what we do (which is awesome!) but we also want to attract new women.  I just want to make sure this is not our only focus.  There are many, many places that we can make a huge difference, this is just one of them.

That said…. 

Two more possible activities for AppSec USA:
Reaching out to a local female tech/programming group to do a coordinated pre-conference event, like they did at AppSec EU.  That was my favourite WIA event to date.  And I bet we made a huge impression on those women, women who were outside of our industry, who may one day decide to join our industry, because of that event.  Because this would be outside of AppSec the deadline would not apply….  We could very easily find them some kick-ass AppSec speakers.  I volunteer to coordinate this activity and even be the speaker if need be.  If someone wants to help me that’s cool too!  Especially if they are local and know people.  Cost for this should be $0, as the meetup would be expected to host…. Or we could ask the local OWASP Chapter to help with this.
Reach out to the local school and teach a mini security lesson to kids or high school. If we could find an all-girls school, this would be ideal.  Again, this was a great idea that the AppSec EU people came up with.   I also think that this probably did not have a large cost…. 


Tanya
