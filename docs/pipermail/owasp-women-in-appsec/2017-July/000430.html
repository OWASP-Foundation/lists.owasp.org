Hi Tanya, Bev,

In the Dublin chapter we're also planning an event in the next few months with the local ISC2 & ISACA chapters to make an impact. The aim would be to influence the local community to seek out female hires and aim for diverse security teams that shift more towards a gender balance rather than the current status quo.

This event will be a follow on from a meeting we had in May last year on 'attracting & retaining women in security' where we had 250 attendees, 2 excellent speakers and a very interesting panel discussion. We had the event recorded and it resonated strongly with the community. We're keen for the next event to do more than highlight the issue and are looking at having an Information Security project competition for different education levels (primary, secondary and third level) for females that will be judged by females to highlight the talent in the industry. We're also trying to get some local companies (Dublin is EMEA HQ for most technology multinationals) to commit to training, internships and to commit to diversity within security teams. We don't want to limit it to "appsec" as the issue is broader and extends to all areas of security.

If we expect the leadership of WIA to have a hand in each event, we will be too slow and limited.  Instead, WIA will provide leadership and support to the many things that our members will do.
