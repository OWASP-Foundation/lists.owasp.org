>> Hello WIA members, this is Tanya Janca, your new chair.
>>
>> I want to change the way we do things.  We are not just an organization
>> that organizes female-friendly events for the AppSec conferences.  We will
>> do much, much more.  This means partnering with other women’s
>> organizations, letting many, many more people join us, media interaction
>> and promotion, and having people run events, from small to large, mostly
>> independently and locally.  If we expect the leadership of WIA to have a
>> hand in each event, we will be too slow and limited.  Instead, WIA will
>> provide leadership and support to the many things that our members will do.
>>
>> I propose the following agenda for our meeting on Tuesday:
>> 1) I will ask for volunteers to coordinate or perform key efforts
>> (someone to approve mailing list messages, someone to lead the AppSec USA
>> effort and someone for each event we want to do and someone to find a
>> female speaker who will be attending c0c0n to do a talk for Shruthi as
>> part of a WIA event).  We have many more key positions, but I will start
>> with those for now as they are time-sensitive.  Please feel free to
>> volunteer for these before the meeting by emailing me.
>> Tanya.Janca at OWASP.org
>> 2) Talk about how I see us moving forward, with activities for all types
>> of members to be active for us and with us.  I want us to be much, much
>> more active at the local level.  I want us to have mini-tasks that anyone
>> can do.  If someone wants to help, no matter who they are, I want them to
>> have something to do.
>> 3) Open Call for ideas moving forward, big and small.  You can email me
>> these directly if you are shy or we run out of time:
>> Tanya.Janca at OWASP.org
>> 4) Homework.  The last 3-5 minutes of each WIA meeting I plan to give
>> everyone optional “homework” that they can consider doing between now and
>> the next meeting.  Items will be something small, such as sharing an
>> article you liked that woman wrote and then tweeting it at the WIA twitter
>> feed.  Homework is always optional, it’s just an idea of something you can
>> do to help for you to consider.
>>
>> Thank you,
>>
>> Tanya Janca
>> (Your new chair)
>>
>> PS In the near future I will be looking for volunteers for the following,
>> please let me know if you are interested:
>> 1) someone to manage partnerships with other women’s groups, this means
>> figuring out when to ask for help and how, plus maintaining a list of who
>> wants to help us.  We have several offers and if you want we could have one
>> person per partnership or one for all.
>> 2) someone to maintain a list of female speakers who are talented and
>> available/willing to do AppSec talks and mention WIA and OWASP, this person
>> will put these women in touch with people looking for female speakers.  The
>> speakers do not need to be members of WIA, but they *must* be OWASP
>> positive/ethical/willing to mention OWASP at some point.
>> 3) men who are willing to do interviews or write an article about why
>> they care about WIA and how it has a positive effect for both men and women
>> when there are more women in AppSec.  I already have an online newspaper
>> interested in doing an article.  Please contact me if you are interested in
>> talking about your experiences!
>> 4) women who are willing to do interviews or write articles that mention
>> OWASP, AppSec, etc.  If you want to mention WIA too, that’s great, but just
>> having female guests on a podcast would be a huge change at this point
>> (many popular podcasts have one < 1 female guest per year).
