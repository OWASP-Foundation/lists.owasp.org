Finally, for the agenda, there are a few items I'd like to have discussed:

1. Status of conversion from Initiative to Committee, hopefully Tiffany can
join and let us know how that's going.  I did see the email from Tiffany
to the BoD about it.

2. AppSec USA 2017.  I've started a conversation with Laura Grau regarding
getting space for WIA and would like to discuss the high-level plans.

3. Scope of the new committee.  Here's the current draft:

The purpose of the Women in AppSec (WIA) committee is to increase the
visibility and participation of women in application security. The scope
for WIA falls into the following areas:

1. Attract women to OWASP, both as members and as participants.
2. Offer opportunities for women to become engaged in the AppSec field and
community.
3. Provide targeted programs for women in high school, college, and recent
college graduates.
4. Provide training and mentorship for women.
5. Provide financial support for women through scholoships and grants.
6. Integrate WIA into OWASP events, including global application security
events.
7. Cultivate women speakers for conferences, trainings, and local events.
8. Develop special projects and events designed to further the purpose of
WIA.

>>> Since July 2014 OWASP has used Committees as the method for members to
>>> drive global change in targeted areas.  Committees have the ability to make
>>> recommendations to the board with a louder voice and request funds in a way
>>> that general groups of people do not have access to.  Because the move from
>>> initiatives to committees has not been facilitated previously, I propose
>>> that we honor the work done by existing active initiatives by accelerating
>>> the process which requires the leader's list show vocal support with no
>>> strong objections.
>>>
>>> This means that the Board will be given notice and a call for members
>>> will be initiated. At this time anyone may join the committee.  This is a
>>> great opportunity to grow your committee attendance.
>>>
>>> There are three types of members in a committee:
>>>
>>> 1) Voting members who are either founding members or invited to become
>>> voting members after having been a participating member for at least 3
>>> months.
>>> 2)Participating members who are able to participate in the discussions
>>> and activities of the committee but not vote
>>> 3)Staff/Board members who are participating members with the added
>>> restriction of not being able to lead the conversation.
>>>
>>> After the process is done, initiatives as they exist now will be totally
>>> phased out.
