> I can speak to the mentor initiative - which is already submitted and
> organized as a committee - it has been an active initiative for about 2
> years and currently 5 participating chapters. As soon as Tiffany gets the
> committee setup with a wiki, we're all set. We already have an active
> mailing list. The mentor committee includes everyone, so if WIA wants to
> have a specialized mentor program just for women, WIA is most welcome to
> participate as a committee with the Mentor Committee. We have our five
> leaders, but recently one dropped, so we need to replace. Would be great to
> have someone from WIA on the leadership of the Mentor Committee, too,
> especially as a liaison. The Mentor Committee started a project about six
> months ago called the Learning Gateway Project. It is also very active. All
> of the Learning Gateway team members attend the monthly Mentor Initiative
> (soon to be Committee) meetings which are held monthly, and posted to the
> OWASP calendar. The next Mentor meeting is currently scheduled for 6pm
> Thursday January 5, 2017. Everyone is welcome to attend. We hope that all
> chapters will send at least one representative from their own local chapter
> mentor programs, as well. We meet on gotomeeting, and all chapters are
> welcome to participate. We also have our local mentor programs here in NY,
> NJ, Long Island and Brooklyn, as well.
>
> I don't think that it is a good idea to limit the time of WIA, so that may
> be a good reason to "not" become a committee and remain an initiative. I
> have expressed previously that I think that we should keep the initiatives,
> and that initiatives should not be forced to become committees. I have not
> seen any interest in the committees since they were presented a few years
> ago. Most members are not interested in committees, but they will support
> an initiative. I think that committees were someone's great idea that was
> never discussed by the members, and was sort of "handed down" from above,
> not really the way that OWASP is supposed to work. I find it amazing that
> since the committee model was imposed, that no one, except myself, and more
> recently, Andrew, has even attempted to create one. I think that the
> committee model is fundamentally flawed and too complex for most people.
> Maybe staff have time for tiresome bureaucratic processes, and they are
> paid for that sort of thing, but most professional volunteers really are
> not interested, nor do we have time for such antics.
>
> Best wishes,
> Bev
