Bev,

I am sorry you feel that my intention is to start everything from the
beginning again.  That must be a miscommunication. As I told you the first
time we spoke about initiatives and committees my plan is to convert them
further along in the process.  Unfortunately, the committee structure is
not new, it predates my hiring by almost 2 years, it was just not
implemented.  Any initiatives who wish to come under the new committee
structure and which are active (active means they can demonstrate that they
are meeting or communicating regularly--regularly is defined by the context
each initiative has set for itself)  will send me their scope and host a
call for members.  If post that call for members the committee has met the
minimum guidelines they will be considered fully converted and expected to
follow the Committees 2.0 guidelines as well as have access to the benefits
of committees.  At that point we will ramp down and redundant structures.

The hope is that by conforming to the official structures of OWASP there
will be less confusion about resources and therefore our community will be
more empowered to continue to develop.    The key here is that we are
looking to improve clarity using already existing tools.

Best,
Tiffany

>>> We definitely should organize as an official committee and I didn't see
>>> anyone object to doing that.  To kick off the process, we need the
>>> following two items:
>>>
>>> 1. We need someone who will send the committee formation email to
>>> the OWASP Leaders List.
>>> 2. For that email, we need the "rationale and desired scope" for the WIA
>>> Committee.  We should hash that out here.
>>>
>>> The Leaders list then discusses the formation of the committee.  I
>>> recommend everyone here join the Leaders list so that you can contribute to
>>> the discussion.  Assuming there are not major objections, it then goes to
>>> the BoD for a vote.  Once the BoD approves, we then need at least five
>>> OWASP members to join the newly formed committee (if you're not a OWASP
>>> member, please join so you can help us here).
>>>
>>> For reference, here is the official process for forming a committee:
>>>
>>> At any point in time, a community member may propose a new committee via
>>> the OWASP Leaders List stating their rationale and desired scope for
>>> creating a new committee. After a community discussion, with perceived
>>> majority support and no major arguments against, the OWASP Board of
>>> Directors will establish whether there is a conflict of interest with any
>>> existing committees and whether the formation of that committee is in line
>>> with with OWASP goals. If no conflict is determined to exist, the Board
>>> will initiate a public call for OWASP members interested in committee
>>> membership, via the OWASP Community mailing list, with a seven day time
>>> window. So long as the committee receives at least five OWASP members
>>> applicants, the Board will vote on the committee creation. A majority vote
>>> of support from the Board is sufficient for establishment of a new
>>> committee with all OWASP member applicants being granted committee
>>> membership.

>>>>>> Hello All,
>>>>>>
>>>>>>
>>>>>> Since July 2014 OWASP has used Committees as the method for members
>>>>>> to drive global change in targeted areas.  Committees have the ability to
>>>>>> make recommendations to the board with a louder voice and request funds in
>>>>>> a way that general groups of people do not have access to.  Because the
>>>>>> move from initiatives to committees has not been facilitated previously, I
>>>>>> propose that we honor the work done by existing active initiatives by
>>>>>> accelerating the process which requires the leader's list show vocal
>>>>>> support with no strong objections.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> This means that the Board will be given notice and a call for members
>>>>>> will be initiated. At this time anyone may join the committee.  This is a
>>>>>> great opportunity to grow your committee attendance.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> There are three types of members in a committee:
>>>>>>
>>>>>> 1) Voting members who are either founding members or invited to
>>>>>> become voting members after having been a participating member for at least
>>>>>> 3 months.
>>>>>> 2)Participating members who are able to participate in the
>>>>>> discussions and activities of the committee but not vote
>>>>>> 3)Staff/Board members who are participating members with the added
>>>>>> restriction of not being able to lead the conversation.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> After the process is done, initiatives as they exist now will be
>>>>>> totally phased out.
>>>>>>
>>>>>> Please let me know if you have any further questions.
