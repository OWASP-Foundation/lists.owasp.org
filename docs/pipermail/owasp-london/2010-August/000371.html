Hi Group

I can 100% say that those stats are not correct. (by this I mean data is
lost via SQL + XSS/CSRF)
I know this as I routinely have reported to companies and websites of data
breeches which involve SQLInjection.

where I have not received a response and the hole remains open I have
contacted UK Info Commissioner at casework at ico.gsi.gov.uk

I have even received responses from them.

" If you believe that any organisation’s website does present a major
security risk as far as personal data are concerned, you can report it to
this office by email and we will assess whether we need to pursue the matter
with the data controller.

 "

So are breeches happening because of WebSecurity holes  = yep.

Are they being reported = yep to Uk Information comissioner and other Govt
Agencies depending on the site.
 (however you'll find that if a company finds or gets notified about an SQL
hole they will generally plug it and *not *notify anybody, they will just
hope that no data got stolen and no one of authority noticed)

There are many people out there who are using custom tools and their learned
skillset to try and warn businesses before the bad guys get in.
We do this for free and in an attempt to try and secure our web integrity.

I'm not 100% sure but I think most countires have seperate groups some govt
funded however most are vonenteer based.

I'd just say that those stats only prove that virtually all organisations
hide the fact that they got owned,
(It hurts the share price to state "we lost 10 milion customer records)

Regards
TheTestManager

> Hi,
> My take on this is that where a physical asset such as a laptop or
> storage media is lost then the organisation is sure that they have
> lost or compromised data. These incidents are therefore reported
> because the data loss is unambiguous.
>
> Where a breach occurs through a web application and there are
> insufficient controls in place to identify the scope of the breach the
> organisation may no know if data has been exfiltrated or how much may
> have been compromised. Ignorance is bliss and in the absence of
> evidence a report is not made. Could this safety blanket potentially
> be a barrier to the adoption data loss monitoring and countermeasure?
>
> You could read this as a success for the security of web applications
> or a failure in the detection of data breaches. I sincerely hope that
> common sense leads the reader to the latter scenario as being most
> likely.
