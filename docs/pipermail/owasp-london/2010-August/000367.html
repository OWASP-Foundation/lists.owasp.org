Hi,
My take on this is that where a physical asset such as a laptop or
storage media is lost then the organisation is sure that they have
lost or compromised data. These incidents are therefore reported
because the data loss is unambiguous.

Where a breach occurs through a web application and there are
insufficient controls in place to identify the scope of the breach the
organisation may no know if data has been exfiltrated or how much may
have been compromised. Ignorance is bliss and in the absence of
evidence a report is not made. Could this safety blanket potentially
be a barrier to the adoption data loss monitoring and countermeasure?

You could read this as a success for the security of web applications
or a failure in the detection of data breaches. I sincerely hope that
common sense leads the reader to the latter scenario as being most
likely.

Regards,
Jim
