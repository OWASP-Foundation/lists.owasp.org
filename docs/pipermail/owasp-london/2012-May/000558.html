>>Following the publication of Claire Perry’s report into online child safety and the subsequent Daily Mail campaign, ISPA and Consumer Focus are organising a meeting to bring together ISPs and civil society organisations.  The purpose of the meeting is to update on where we are and look at potential scope for future work and messaging, particularly with the Comms Green Paper likely to touch on this.  
>> 
>>For instance, the freedom of expression implications for default filtering are important but often sound better coming from civil society organisations rather than ISPs.  Alongside ISPA, Big Brother Watch and ORG have been criticising the plans, but it would be good to get more organisations raising concerns.
>>
