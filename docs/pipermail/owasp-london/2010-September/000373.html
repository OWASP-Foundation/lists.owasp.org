Sadly, I think you're probably right.  I also agree with Jim that this is
likely largely an information problem. Firstly, and organisation may not
even be aware that they have had a breach from a web application attack.
Also, in the case that they are aware of a breach they may not be able to
quantify the problem through lack of logged information.  I've only come
across one instance where an organisation we've worked with had a good
record of exactly what data was exposed, and even in that case it was
largely blind luck that they had been logging that information...

In the case that an organisation with my data is aware they've had a breach
that they can't quantify I'd like to see them assume that the information
has been exposed and act accordingly, but do they see it that way?
