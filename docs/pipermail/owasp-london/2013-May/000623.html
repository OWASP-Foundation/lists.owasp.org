> Synopsis
> 
> 15 nations, NATO and EU organisations have just completed Multinational
> Experiment 7 (MNE7) to "ensure access to the Global Commons". The largest
> activity was around cyberspace.
> 
> MNE7's experimentation demonstrated how collaboration across organisations
> dramatically increased the local and collective ability to deal with threats
> and incidents, and ensure the normal availability of cyberspace. The
> participating nations have decided to develop and implement the Information
> Sharing Framework (ISF) for Collaborative Cyber Situational Awareness
> (CCSA), and stand up a new organisation to enable this to happen - the
> Multinational Alliance for CCSA (MACCSA) - involving governments, industry
> and military.
> 
> Patrick Curry will describe the key components in the ISF, how they are
> being implemented and a bit about the business and regulatory drivers. He
> will focus on what technologies will be affected and the new enabling
> technologies already being deployed.
> 
> Neil Hare-Brown will present the benefits of implementing IODEF and also
> talk about the need for an effective incident response process.
