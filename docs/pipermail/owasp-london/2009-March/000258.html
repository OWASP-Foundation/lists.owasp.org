I have a project that might interest people, here is the oulines:


Secure communication Framework

The goal is to offer a secure layer of
communication using a well managed PKI, to tiers applications, users
or systems. This layer would be on the top of any operating system,
and just under the application layer.
This would be a multi-platform software
( Windows, Mac OS, Linux, embeded OS)
The general system would be highly
distributed, without real central point, apart from some specialized
components, like the certification authority.

The main parts would be : 
	* Automated certificate generation,
distribution and revocation (as far as it is applicable).
	* Integrated authentication via
certificate.
	* Messaging protocol offered for tier
applications, this would include a complete routing system and a naming
system.
	* Possibility for any application to
use the certificate or to use a secure channel from the framework.
	* multi-platform: Windows, Mac OS,
Linux, embeded OS, ... (Mainly due to the use of Qt framework: http://trolltech.com/products/qt/ )

Potentials applications:
	* Secure Instant messaging
	* Secure VOIP
	* Secure administration of complex
network, from user stations to servers, including switches, firewall,
routers.
	* User mobility management (laptop,
phone, PDA)
Let me know if that sounds good, and if this funding is also open to
non-UK people (french as a matter of fact)

I thought this may be of interest to some people on the list:

  Information Infrastructure Protection: Managing complexity, risk
and resilience
  MARCH 2009 COMPETITION FOR FUNDING
  http://www.innovateuk.org/_assets/pdf/competitioninformationinfrastructprotection1.pdf

>From the document:

"Projects should make real progress towards meeting the targets set
out in the UK Government's National Information Assurance Strategy and
mitigating electronic risks cited in the National Risk Register. These
take the form of clear and effective risk management; provision of the
right 'tools' for organisations to protect themselves; protection of
critical infrastructures from electronic attack; and enhanced public,
commercial, and industrial confidence in the UK's ability to manage
and protect information."

"All proposals must explain how the work will help in positioning the
UK in the global market and against other global innovation leaders in
the area. Projects should be mindful of how they might contribute to
the European Commission ICT Objective 1.4 Secure, Dependable and
Trusted Infrastructures and Objective 1.7 Critical Infrastructure
Protection."

The National Risk Register is available at:

  http://www.cabinetoffice.gov.uk/reports/national_risk_register.aspx
