On 27 Jan 2012, at 12:57, Paco Hope wrote:

> I'm happy to put some effort in, and I can put a face on it if need 
> be.
>
> To inspire secure software I think we need to inspire thoroughly 
> correct software. When software does exactly what it says on the tin, 
> with no unexpected behaviours, it also happens to be secure. 
> Developers don't write software that doesn't do stuff. They write 
> software that does stuff. They must be told how to do the job well.
>
> If I had to pick 3 points, one of them would be from the test-driven 
> development camp. Basically provoking every developer who says her 
> software/module/function does X to provide some reason (e.g., a unit 
> test, integration test, etc.) for believing it. In this high tech 
> world, we are entirely too prone to believe things work, without 
> applying a healthy filter of skepticism. Children are brought up 
> amongst technology that looks like magic to OAPs. They see it work 
> over and over and don't develop the skepticism that they should. They 
> should be taught that software fails, hardware fails, and people make 
> mistakes. Testing while developing is vital for catching problems 
> before they grow into larger issues. I will bet that many learning 
> computer science today struggle to write a clear test case that tests 
> exactly what they need to test, and nothing else.
>
> My £0.02,
> Paco
