I am pleased to announce we have Scott Stender of iSec Partners speaking
at our upcoming Thursday night OWASP meeting. The presentation topic is
Web Services Security. Scott will discuss how deploying a web
services-based architecture introduces a number of attack services that
need to be well understood and protected against misuse.

As we are having food and beverages served, please RSVP so I can give an
accurate count to the restaurant catering. The info below will be
updated on the OWASP Seattle web site this evening.

following

Las Margaritas Restaurant

tel: (425) 453-0535

Food, including vegetarian fare, and beverages will be served. The
meeting will be held in a private meeting room at Las Margaritas.

Presentation: Web Services Security
Featured Speaker: Scott Stender, iSec Partners

Scott is a founding partner of iSEC Partners, LLC, a strategic digital
security organization. Scott brings several years of experience in
software development and security consulting. Prior to iSEC, he worked
as an application security analyst with @stake where he led and
delivered on many of @stake's highest priority clients. In his research,
he focuses on secure software engineering methodology and security
analysis of core technologies. Most recently, Scott was published in the
Jan/Feb 2005 issue of "IEEE Security & Privacy," where he co-authored a
paper entitled "Software Penetration Testing." He holds a BS in Computer
Engineering from the University of Notre Dame.
