The government's Central Office of Information (COI) public consultation on browser standards for public websites is undergoing consultation:

   COI - Browser Standards Consultation
   http://www.coi.gov.uk/guidance.php?page=200
   
Whilst the document seems primarily to be discussing usability, the requirements for testing a public sector site "works in common browsers" are to check: 

   1 content
   2 functionality and
   3 display.

Nothing is mentioned about security.

There's already plenty of feedback from elsewhere about the choice of browsers, standards, etc:

   http://www.webstandards.org/2008/09/08/uk-government-draft-browser-guidance-is-daft-browser-guidance/
 
   http://www.thepickards.co.uk/index.php/200809/public-sector-browser-standards/

   http://my.opera.com/ODIN/blog/response-browser-standards-consultation

but perhaps OWASP London and/or OWASP Scotland could submit comments on the draft document, or we could submit a joint response about our particular interest - security?  I'm not thinking we should comment on the selection of browsers, but rather making sure that security is considered during testing.  After all, some browsers and versions which are popular may mean the user is more at risk from vulnerabilities on the web site?

Colin Watson
