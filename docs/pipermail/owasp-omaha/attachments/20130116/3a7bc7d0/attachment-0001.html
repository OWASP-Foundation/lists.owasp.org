          <div dir="ltr">
            <div>Forwarding for our OWASP Omaha members:<br>
              <br>
              The Colorado <a moz-do-not-send="true"
                href="https://www.owasp.org" target="_blank">OWASP</a> chapters
              are pleased to announce that the 5th annual Front Range
              OWASP Conference (<a moz-do-not-send="true"
                href="http://www.snowfroc.com" target="_blank">SnowFROC</a>)
              will occur on Thursday, March 28, 2013 in Denver,
              Colorado, USA.</div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div>Effective immediately, the SnowFROC planning committee
              is accepting proposals for presentations on topics
              surrounding cyber security and web application development
              and operations.</div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div>
              <b><u>Criteria and Submission</u></b></div>
            <div>Potential speakers may submit abstracts of proposed
              presentations to <a moz-do-not-send="true"
                href="https://dev.aerstone.com/snowfroc/cfp"
                target="_blank">https://dev.aerstone.com/snowfroc/cfp</a>.</div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div>The submission tool will format newly-created abstracts
              automatically, but all uploaded materials must adhere to
              the following requirements:</div>
            <div>
              <ul>
                <li>Only Microsoft Word documents, PDFs, rich-text
                  format documents, and text files will be accepted.<br>
                </li>
                <li>Author names, affiliations, email addresses, and
                  other personally-identifiable information must be
                  stripped from uploaded documents.<br>
                </li>
                <li>Presentations must contain a title at the top of the
                  document.<br>
                </li>
                <li>Presentation overviews should not exceed 300 words.<br>
                </li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div>Presenters will be allotted 45-minute time slots. One
              abstract must be submitted for each presentation
              considered. All abstracts must be written in English.<br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><b><u>Evaluation</u></b></div>
            <div>The following criteria will determine abstract
              rankings:</div>
            <div>
              <ul>
                <li>Applicability to the requested topics (see below)<br>
                </li>
                <li>Relevance to web application development and
                  operations<br>
                </li>
                <li>Relevance to the cybersecurity Industry at large<br>
                </li>
                <li>Relevance to the OWASP foundation<br>
                </li>
                <li>Strength of presentation (as determined by the
                  review committee)<br>
                </li>
                <li>Timeliness of submission</li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div>Submitted abstracts will be assessed by selected
              members of the Colorado OWASP chapters. All reviews will
              be blind and reviewers will not have visibility to author
              information.<br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><b><u>Dates and Deadlines</u></b></div>
            <div>
              <div>Abstract collection begins immediately and will
                continue until all speaking slots are filled. Rolling
                evaluations will occur with selected papers announced
                each Monday beginning on February 11th. Although the
                rolling format extends the submission period
                significantly, potential speakers are advised to submit
                as early as possible in order to maximize chances for
                selection.</div>
              <div><br>
              </div>
              <div>Final presentations of accepted abstracts must be
                submitted for review by March 17th. Templates and other
                presentation formatting constraints will be made
                available to selected speakers directly.</div>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div>All accepted presentations will be delivered during
              conference proceedings on March 28th, 2013.</div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><b><u>Legalities</u></b></div>
            <div>All speakers must agree with and abide by the <a
                moz-do-not-send="true"
                href="https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Speaker_Agreement"
                target="_blank">OWASP Speaker Agreement v2.0</a>.</div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><b>Anyone who cannot or will not abide by these terms
                will not be permitted to present at the conference.</b></div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div>In addition, presenters must agree to allow use of
              abstract titles, text, and speaker names and bios for
              conference promotion. With speaker consent, presentation
              materials will be distributed to conference attendees and
              will be archived for future reference. With speaker
              consent, presentations will be recorded and archived.</div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><br>
            </div>
            <div><b><u>Preferred Topics</u></b></div>
            <div>
              <div>SnowFROC 2013 is a multi-track conference emphasizing
                a breadth of information and skills. Conference
                organizers are actively seeking presentations aligning
                with the following tracks and topics:</div>
              <div><br>
              </div>
              <div><b>High-level technical track</b></div>
              <div>
                <ul>
                  <li>Web application security testing, especially
                    targeting large and complex enterprise applications<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Mobile device security</li>
                  <li>Cloud security<br>
                  </li>
                  <li><span style="white-space:pre-wrap"> Impacts on the
                    </span>security model<br>
                  </li>
                  <li><span style="white-space:pre-wrap"> </span>Implementation
                    of security controls<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>OWASP tools and projects<br>
                  </li>
                  <li><span style="white-space:pre-wrap"> </span>New
                    and proposed projects<br>
                  </li>
                  <li><span style="white-space:pre-wrap"> </span>Development
                    and status of existing projects<br>
                  </li>
                </ul>
              </div>
              <div><br>
              </div>
              <div><b>Deep-dive technical track</b></div>
              <div>
                <ul>
                  <li>Technology-specific presentations (HTML5, AJAX,
                    etc.)<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Secure coding for web applications<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Static code analysis<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Hands-on countermeasures<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Encryption across an n-tiered web application
                    stack<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Auditing web application mash-ups</li>
                </ul>
              </div>
              <div><br>
              </div>
              <div><b>Management track</b></div>
              <div>
                <ul>
                  <li>Web application security management (scope,
                    boundaries, responsibilities, legal considerations,
                    etc.)<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Emerging threats<br>
                  </li>

                  </li>

                  </li>

              </div>
              <div><br>
              </div>
              <div><b>Legal track</b></div>
              <div>
                <ul>
                  <li>Liability related to web application security<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Data ownership and privacy laws within the cloud<br>
                  </li>
                  <li>Cybersecurity and privacy legislation and
                    regulation</li>
                  <li>Electronic discovery considerations, both
                    traditional and in the cloud<br>
                  </li>
