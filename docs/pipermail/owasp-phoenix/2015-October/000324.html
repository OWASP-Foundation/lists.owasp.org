Dear *Owasp-phoenix Subscribers,*

I'm happy to announce our next OWASP Phoenix chapter meeting, which will be
held at Early Warning on Thursday, November 05, 2015.  Joe Giron will be
presenting on Reverse Engineering Malware for Newbies.

I apologize again for the long badging process, I have been assured that
for those of you that have already been through the temp badge process, it
will much faster this time.  Even further, if you RSVP through the meetup
site, I will ask for your badge to be pre-printed.

Notably, this date worked out best for our speaker's schedule.  I hope that
the day and venue allows members to attend that had schedule or location
challenges.  I'm sure this also created conflicts for some of you, so
apologies, and we look forward to having you join us at future meetings.

Regards, Joaquin

If you use Meetup.com, please RSVP so that I know how many potential
attendees we might have.  http://www.meetup.com/owasp-phoenix

Details have been posted to the Chapter Page:
https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Phoenix

Thursday, Nov 05
Time: 6:30 - 7:30 (arrive about 15 minutes early)
Location: Early Warning 16552 N 90th St Ste 100, Scottsdale, AZ 85260
Room: Checkin with Security Officer, picture ID required
Title: *Reverse Engineering Malware for Newbies*
Presenter(s): *Joe Giron*
In this talk we're going to cover basic malware analysis, unpacking 101,
dynamic analysis, memory analysis, where to get malware, basic x86/64 ASM,
tools of the trade, setting up an environment, intro to the debugger, basic
debugger usage, and reporting.

About the presenter: Joe Giron is a 29 year old security enthusiast and
Phoenix native. When not hacking the planet, he can be found at home
writing video game cheats or working on his truck. He also enjoys candle
lit dinners and long walks on the beach.

For first timers, please make sure to arrive at least 15 minutes early due
to the required badging registration. A picture ID is required to enter the
building.
