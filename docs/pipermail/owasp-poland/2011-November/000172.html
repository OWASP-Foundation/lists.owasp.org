Interesting indeed, although I don't agree on several things as some assumptions are totally wrong.

The most relevant comments are:
- pentesting is all about expertise and experience of the team. Comparing a test performed by few testers (which also include students) does not seem a reliable assumption

- one of the application had something like ~1000 SQL injections. It suggests that the app has never been audited thus there were several low hanging fruits just waiting to be discovered with automated tools. As a result, the target was indeed not representative.

Having said that, I agree with combining approaches for getting as many bugs as possible. Also, I support the conclusion that "different techniques bring different bugs". However, we can't say that it's supported by scientific results.
