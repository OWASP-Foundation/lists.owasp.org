> Further to your request for assistance with making the text of A7 more
> inclusive of our project's automated threats, as well as all four of the
> issues you mentioned.
>
> WE FEEL THAT INCLUDING OTHER RISKS IN A7 TOGETHER WITH "Insufficient
> Anti-Automation", DILUTES THIS IMPORTANT COMMON AND EASILY EXPLOITABLE
> RISK, AND MAY INCREASE CONFUSION ABOUT HOW TO ADDRESS THE VARIOUS ISSUES.
> HOWEVER, FOR THE PURPOSES OF THIS REPLY WE ASSUME A7 WILL CONTINUE TO BE
> THE STATED AGGREGATION OF RISKS.
>
> We have not attempted to address anyone else's concerns with A7, but
> should note that our project is technology and vendor agnostic, and our
> suggested changes to the wording of A7 avoid the mention of specific
> technologies, or products, or services. We welcome the Top Ten project's
> attempt to raise awareness of automated threat weaknesses/vulnerabilities
> that often go un-mentioned, yet are of real concern, and impact, to
> application owners.
>
> 1. Full page text
>
> We realise there are constraints on space, and therefore we have mocked up
> an updated page to ensure our suggested changes would fit in. Changes
> sections are marked in the colour magenta in the PNG at
> https://www.owasp.org/index.php/File:Owasp-a7-suggestions.png
>
> 2. Short description
>
> The short description of A7 on page 7 of RC1 does not seem to include all
> the issues A7 is meant to include. It is currently "The majority of
> applications and APIs lack the basic ability to detect, prevent, and
> respond to both manual and automated attacks. Attack protection goes far
> beyond basic input validation and involves automatically detecting,
> logging, responding, and even blocking exploit attempts. Application owners
> also need to be able to deploy patches quickly to protect against
> attacks.". BASED ON YOUR OTHER EMAILS TO THE TOP TEN LIST, our project
> believes it might be better as: "The majority of applications and APIs lack
> the basic ability to detect, prevent and respond to manual and automated
> attacks. This includes insufficient attack detection, insufficient attack
> response, insufficient countermeasures against automated threats, and
> insufficient ability to patch quickly."
>
> 3. Title
>
> Whilst of course we would have liked to see "Insufficient Anti-Automation"
> as a single issue, we accept your project's desire to aggregate this with
> some other issues. We do not mind too much the name "Insufficient Attack
> Protection" but it may suggest to some it is more of an operational thing,
> rather than encompassing the many countermeasures that can be considered
> through the whole of the software development life cycle. Yes there are
> design flaws and implementation bugs in this item!  Our project has not
> been able to come up with a SUGGESTED better title that includes all the
> aspects to be considered. Most Top Ten issues are 3-4 words long, although
> we note that A1 is just "Injection". One suggestion - which we feel is weak
> - is simply to name A7 "Automation", but this is a poor munging together of
> meaning: a) Automated attacks and b) Automation of detection and response
> defenses [to both manual and automated attacks]. However, it does remove
> any other descriptive and thus judgemental words.

>>> Colin & Tin -
>>>
>>> I think you've done some great work here - thank you for taking the lead
>>> and putting energy into this important topic.
>>>
>>> It is hard to know the best way to properly frame this issue so that it
>>> makes sense in the context of the top 10. I am worried that as currently
>>> written, A7 is too confusing to produce much benefit for security
>>> practitioners. I consider the following two facets of this topic to be the
>>> most important:
>>>
>>> *1. Attacks rely on legitimate functionality:*
>>> the 'attacks' in almost all cases involve requests which are valid and
>>> legitimate (such as a credential stuffing attack, which involves seemingly
>>> valid login requests). This is a major shift towards such attacks because
>>> of the widespread availability of useful data (like usernames/passwords).
>>>
>>> *2. Attacks require automation:*
>>> Few attacks which rely solely on legitimate functionality produce a
>>> benefit if performed manually. Most require automation in order to produce
>>> economic ROI (which is the primary sustained persistent reason for these
>>> types of attacks). Currently A7 continues to mention "and manual" which I
>>> think is a major flaw. While "manual" attacks against legitimate
>>> functionality are certainly a possibility, they are not in practice causing
>>> the kinds of problems that automated attacks are causing.
>>>
>>> I have some specific thoughts on your bullets 1-3 which I'll share
>>> later, but wanted to share these initial thoughts now.
>>>
>>> Thanks,
>>> Sumit

>>>> =================================================

>>>> Dave
>>>>
>>>> Further to your request for assistance with making the text of A7 more
>>>> inclusive of our projects automated threats, as well as all four of the
>>>> issues you mentioned.
>>>>
>>>> We have not attempted to address anyone else's concerns with A7, but
>>>> should note that our project is technology and vendor agnostic, and our
>>>> suggested changes to the wording of A7 avoid the mention of specific
>>>> technologies, or products, or services. We welcome the Top Ten project's
>>>> attempt to raise awareness of automated threat weaknesses/vulnerabilities
>>>> that often go un-mentioned, yet are of real concern, and impact, to
>>>> application owners.
>>>>
>>>> 1. Full page text
>>>>
>>>> We realise there are constraints on space, and therefore we have mocked
>>>> up an updated page to ensure our suggested changes would fit in. Changes
>>>> sections are marked in the colour magenta in the PNG at
>>>> https://www.owasp.org/index.php/File:Owasp-a7-suggestions.png
>>>>
>>>> 2. Short description
>>>>
>>>> The short description of A7 on page 7 of RC1 does not seem to include
>>>> all the issues A7 is meant to include. It is currently "The majority of
>>>> applications and APIs lack the basic ability to detect, prevent, and
>>>> respond to both manual and automated attacks. Attack protection goes far
>>>> beyond basic input validation and involves automatically detecting,
>>>> logging, responding, and even blocking exploit attempts. Application owners
>>>> also need to be able to deploy patches quickly to protect against
>>>> attacks.". Our project believes it might be better as: "The majority of
>>>> applications and APIs lack the basic ability to detect, prevent and respond
>>>> to manual and automated attacks. This includes insufficient attack
>>>> detection, insufficient attack response, insufficient countermeasures
>>>> against automated threats, and insufficient ability to patch quickly."
>>>>
>>>> 3. Title
>>>>
>>>> Whilst of course we would have liked to see "Insufficient
>>>> Anti-Automation" as a single issue, we accept your project's desire to
>>>> aggregate this with some other issues. We do not mind too much the name
>>>> "Insufficient Attack Protection" but it may suggest to some it is more of
>>>> an operational thing rather than encompassing the many countermeasures that
>>>> can be considered through the whole of the software development life cycle.
>>>> Yes there are design flaws and implementation bugs in this item!  Our
>>>> project has not been able to come up with a better title that includes all
>>>> the aspects to be considered. Most Top Ten issues are 3-4 words long,
>>>> although we note that A1 is just "Injection". One suggestion - which we
>>>> feel is weak - is simply to name A7 "Automation", but this is a poor
>>>> munging together of meaning: a) Automated attacks and b) Automation of
>>>> detection and response defenses [to both manual and automated attacks].
>>>> However, it does remove any other descriptive and thus judgemental words.
>>>>
