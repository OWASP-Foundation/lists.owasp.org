KC-OWASP,

I got called out of state for work so can't make it to today's panel.

WSJ news from today that Intel disclosed Spectre and Meltdown to the
Chinese before Americans is very sobering, well, maybe more like
infuriating:

https://www.wsj.com/articles/intel-warned-chinese-companies-of-chip-flaws-before-u-s-government-1517157430

Advanced knowledge of this flaw would enable attackers to effectively
bypass many many cloud and device controls and China has the resources to
do it.
Consider the dep history of Chinese presence at pwn2own in Canada as
evidence of capability.
Intel's in-you-face lack of disclosure aligned with national interests is a
shot heard round the world.  More interested in their global interests than
America first?  Let's see how that plays out.

Proposed format for today's panel discussion:

1. What do we know about how these flaws (similiarities and differences to
other chip-level timing attacks like rowhammer)
2. What mitigations are available from Intel, Apple, Microsoft, Google, and
others.  Do they work?  Which mitigations provide greater strength in
combination (browser+OS+container).
3. Devices affected: ARM?  x86?  see:
https://www.techarp.com/guides/complete-meltdown-spectre-cpu-list/
4. Finally, what is the expected impact to corporate IT, software
developers, and the industry as a whole?  And where do we go from here?

Cheers,


Mat
