> Google's Site Isolation
> <https://www.chromium.org/Home/chromium-security/site-isolation> and
> presumably similar patches in Apple's Safari later this month will reduce
> the likelihood of using the browser as an attack vector.  However, the fact
> that browser level patches are being rolled out indicates that at this
> time, browser-based languages such as javascript are a potential means of
> exploit for these pervasive flaws.
>
> Due to browser involvement, the OWASP community i.e.software security
> community, have a distinct role to play in this response, both in terms of
> increasing awareness and advisement around mitigations and potential
> technical and non-technical responses to the issues raised.
>
> Can we set up a meeting this month or next month wherein we can assemble
> to discuss technical briefings and mitigation strategies?  The Kaufman
> Center seems like an ideal place to hold such a meeting and it would be
> good to make requests of Kansas City's infosec community leaders to attend
> and contribute where possible.
>
>

>
>

> potentially affected, which is effectively every processor since 1995
> (except Intel Itanium and Intel Atom before 2013)
>
> Linux mitigation is KAISER / KPTI for the kernel.
> Vendor patches for OSX and Windows versions are underway or already
> delivered.
>
>
