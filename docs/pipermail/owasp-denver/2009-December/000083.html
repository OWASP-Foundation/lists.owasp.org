*December **ISACA Denver **Chapter Meeting*
Thursday, December 17, 2009
*Improving IT Governance:  Making Life Better for All of Us*
 
ISACA Denver Chapter is pleased to announce an interesting topic for
December monthly meeting form Bob Frelinger, CGEIT, IT Management Cycle
Program Manager, Sun Microsystems, Inc. This session is open to both the
members of ISACA Denver Chapter and non-members. Our December meeting
will include a presentation and a panel discussion comprising SMEs and
industry practitioners in the IT Governance area. Please review the
content below for additional registration details.
CPE Credits: 1.5
 
*Who should attend?*
Internal Auditors
Security Professionals
IT Governance Professionals
Security and Auditing Managers
CISOs
CIOs
IT Development Managers
 
*Topic Summary *
Regardless of our role in the enterprise, we each have a stake in how
the IT contribution is governed.  The better that enterprise IT is
governed and managed, the better we each can do our jobs. And no matter
our primary role...whether that be audit, assurance, compliance, risk,
security, IT or business...we each have a role in improving the overall
governance and management of IT.  During our program, we will talk about
specific ways that we each can, and should, be involved in IT
governance.   And, with the help of panelists, we'll explore specific
ways we each can contribute to dealing with some of the common issues
that organizations encounter when working to implement, improve, and
sustain their IT governance and management work.

*About the Speaker*
 
Bob Frelinger, CGEIT, is the IT Management Cycle Program Manager at Sun
Microsystems, Inc. Since mid-2003, he has been working with CobiT and
introducing its concepts into Sun IT. He is the recipient of ISACA's
2005 "Michael Cangemi Best Article Award" for his case study, "Building
Acceptance and Adoption of CobiT at Sun Microsystems," which appeared in
volume 2, 2005, of the Information Systems Control Journal.

Bob came to the IT industry after a 24-year military career where he
specialized in human resource management and administration. Immediately
following his military service, he spent four years advising/training
the Saudi Arabian National Guard on how to automate their human resource
management systems. He has been with Sun Microsystems since 1999.  He is
a CISA and holds certifications in COBIT, ITIL, PRINCE2, and Six Sigma.
  Bob was an active contributor to CobiT 4.0 and 4.1 and to the
ISACA/ITGI document titled, "IT Control Objectives for Sarbanes-Oxley:
The Role of the IT in the Design and Implementation of Internal Controls
Over Financial Reporting, 2nd Edition". He is an ISACA accredited
trainer for the "Implementing IT Governance using COBIT and Val IT"
course.   He served as a member of the ISACA IT Governance Credential
Task Force (2006-2007), on the CGEIT Certification Board (2007-2008),
and on the Program Committee for ISACA's 2007 IT Governance and
Compliance Conference.  Bob has a Masters in Information Systems from
the University of Colorado at Denver.
