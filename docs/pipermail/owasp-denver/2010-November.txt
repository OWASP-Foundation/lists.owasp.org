Topic Summary

1. Cloud Computing Security by Matt McMillon, Staff information Security Engineer, Technology Management, Qwest Communications
Matt is planning to address basic knowledge about cloud computing, cloud computing threat models, detailed overview of vulnerabilities in the cloud environment. This presentation will address issues from both a customer and service provider perspective, a soup to nuts overview of cloud computing compliance and security issues based on Cloud Security Alliance (CSA) and European Network and Information Security Agency (ENISA) recommendations.
2. Virtualization and Cloud Computing Overview - Risk and Auditing Challenges
Topic Objective
With the emergence of virtualization and cloud computing, connectivity and collaboration become the key enablers in the next generation's enterprise technology architecture.   But is your infrastructure ready to respond to the risks, and truly enable this transformation?
Synopsis
As both users and applications increasingly rely on connectivity, collaboration and computing power to achieve new levels of functionality and productivity, emerging cloud computing services and virtualization technologies offer great opportunities in transforming the face of IT, with the potential to deliver real business value.

A holistic enterprise technology architecture that fully exploits the strength of the application and the intelligent infrastructure is required to fully realize the potential of a collaborative environment, as well as to avoid potential pitfalls in areas such as data integrity, recovery, privacy, e-discovery, regulatory compliance, and auditing.
This presentation will provide an overview of these emerging technologies concepts and provide a high level view of potential risks.
Speaker Bio:

McMillon, Staff information Security Engineer, Technology Management, Qwest Communications

Matt McMillon is a Staff Internet Engineer with Qwest Communications specializing in securing emerging technologies, including VoIP, IPTV and Cloud Computing.
Matt advocates a methodical approach to information security that involves threat modeling, quantifying business goals of technology, and the understanding that there is no such thing as absolute security.
Prior to his 8-year career in information security, Matt was corporate Webmaster for U S WEST and Qwest Communications and produced Web sites for other high profile clients including the Salt Lake City Olympic Organizing Committee and Autodesk.

Amr Ahmed
Ernst & Young

 Advisory Services, Senior Manager

Amr Ahmed is IT Advisory Senior Manager for Ernst & Young he regularly provides strategic technology architecture advisory for global enterprises, promoting advanced service in terms of technical services that better align IT capabilities with the corporate strategy.    Amr spent the last 10 years focusing on enterprise technology architecture, and data center  / disaster recovery strategy.   He is a frequent speaker regarding emerging technologies (e.g. SOA, Cloud Computing, Virtualization), Infrastructure Resiliency and Data Center strategy.   He has presented at industry events such as MPLScon, FutureNet, FS-ISAC, and others.

ISACA Denver Chapter and IAOP (International Association of Outsourcing Professional) are partnering deliver a comprehensive one day outsourcing conference addressing information security, IT auditing, compliance and governance challenges associated with outsourcing and off-shoring. The conference promises a day of education through discussions with industry-leading Outsourcing IT, Telecom and Network service providers, Cloud Computing service providers, Managed Security Service providers and local information security, audit, compliance and IT professionals.
