Intangible assets:

If we bought a potato peeler that required so many updates, tweeks etc we
would ask for our money back.  We cant touch, see, smell or feel much of the
issues with software today but they affect of lives nonetheless.



In the case of software we accept this as the norm; "expect patches"
(otherwise knows as "yes software is developed with low quality/security in
mind") or "everybody is doing this so it must be ok".



*We never fix our mistakes and we keep creating new ones*. -  Should be on
the tombstone for the software development industry.



The CVE list grows every year. IRISS is busier than ever and next year
busier methinks. Sure why do you think anyone involved in Secure SDLC,
Secure Code, App Sec is slammed with work?  The bad guys aren't only
stealing the cars, they are owning the roads as well.
