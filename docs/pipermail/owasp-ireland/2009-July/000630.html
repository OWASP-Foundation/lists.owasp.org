Surely the argument is simple, give the man in the street a reason to 
upgrade and he will. If sites such as the BBC and Facebook stop working 
for IE6 users they will upgrade, we have had two IE releases, god knows 
how many Firefox releases, Safari to Windows and Google Chrome since the 
IE6 release date yet quite obviously none of them have made a 
significant (to the man in the street) difference to the  web browsing 
experience otherwise people would have an incentive to move.

If you want to innovate then innovate, if your innovation is good enough 
then people will upgrade their browser to use it, if they don't need to 
upgrade then they won't. Crying about it won't achieve anything ;-)

Dave
