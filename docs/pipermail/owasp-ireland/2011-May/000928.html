OWASP is having a couple of speakers at the ISSA conference next Wed/Thur.

David Stubley from 7 elements (http://7elements.co.uk/) is talking about APT
(Advanced Persistent Threat) and pixy dust.

Eoin Keary (OWASP Global Director) shall be talking about Code
security/SDLC/Code review and securifying the interweb.
See below:



ISSA Ireland - Conference 2011 (Wed May 11 & Thur May 12)
*


ISSA Ireland is delighted to present the
*programme*<http://www.issaireland.org/conference/ISSAIrelandConference-DraftProgramme.pdf>for
our two day Information Security Conference. We have some fantastic
international speakers lined up along with world class keynotes: Ed Gibson,
Fred Piper and Dennis Jennings speaking respectively on:

Cyber Fact or Fiction?: Insiders, Nation States, organized criminals

Information Security: Is it in a healthy state?

Internet Governance: past, present and future

The event will break into multiple tracks on both days to allow delegates to
attend specialist talks of their choice. The main track is top-level
international speakers on diverse topics:

Selecting an SIEM vendor/solution

Chip and Pin Internals

On-line Privacy Controls: What they don't say

8 days a week - behind the Verizon Business Data Breach Investigations
Report

Productivity vs Control: End-user Adoption of Online Collaboration Services

PICO - no more passwords

PCI DSS 2.0: What happens next!

Fixing your passwords the easy way

A specialist track follows specialist themes of OWASP, Digital Forensics,
Systems Audit, and Public Sector each led by an acknowledged subject matter
expert. A side track includes a hands-on workshop on the Maltego online
research tool and a SANS GIAC re-certification exam. Conference attendance
will qualify for CPE credits (usually one credit per hour attendance). On
the Wednesday, there will be a limited seating dinner attended by one of the
keynotes.

Discounted ticket prices are available for ISACA and OWASP members (EUR100
plus booking fee) and members of IISF, IRISS and some other professional
organisations (EUR125 plus booking fee). Conference attendance will qualify
for CPE credits (usually one credit per hour attendance).

OWASP Discount Code: *
http://www.eventbrite.com/event/1407687431?discount=OWASP*<http://www.eventbrite.com/event/1407687431?discount=OWASP>

Non-members looking for the best rate should consider joining ISSA at the
ISSA International website *www.issa.org* <http://www.issa.org/> (and then
select "Join Now"). Membership of the Irish Chapter costs US$250 (about
EUR175) which includes your conference ticket.
