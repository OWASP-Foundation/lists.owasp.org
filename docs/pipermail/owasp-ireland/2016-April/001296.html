Hi Everyone,

We're happy to announce that we will be hosting the next OWASP Dublin
chapter event in the Morgan McKinley’s office on Thursday May 26th 2016 at
6:30pm.

The cybersecurity industry has significant shortage of skilled
professionals regionally, nationally and internationally. Research has
shown that women’s representation in cybersecurity has been low and is on
decline. This OWASP Dublin chapter meeting aims to discuss many of the
challenges around attracting and retaining women in the cybersecurity
industry. We're very exited to have the guests below who will speak on the
topic and give their insights.

Please feel free to forward the event to friends and colleagues.

Please use the link below to register:
https://www.eventbrite.com/e/attracting-retaining-women-in-the-cybersecurity-tickets-24970640859

We're hoping to have some gifts to give away as part of the night.

*Session 1 - Jacky Fox*
Jacky joined the team in Deloitte in 2012 bringing 20 years of Irish and
international IT consulting experience. She previously worked with large
American PC manufacturing corporations serving clients from both corporate
and public sectors. Jacky leads the Cyber Security and Digital Forensics
teams in Ireland. Jacky is very focused on meeting client needs and with
her IT industry experience can offer well rounded advice and guidance in
challenging situations. Jacky has worked with several large clients in
Ireland and abroad across multiple sectors providing advice and direction
preparing for, protecting from and reacting to Cyber Security incidents.
Jacky has an MSc in Digital Investigation and Forensic Computing and holds
a GCFA. She has published several research and thought leadership articles
and lectures in Cyber Security at UCD.

*Session 2 - Jane Frankland*
Jane Frankland is a successful cyber security entrepreneur, author, speaker
and business consultant. She is also a Board Advisor for ClubCISO, a
private forum for over 140 senior cyber security FTSE leaders, an SC
Magazine Awards Judge and has been nominated for Cyber Security Woman of
the Year 2016. She has built and sold her own seven-figure global cyber
security and penetration testing consultancy and is frequently invited to
speak about entrepreneurship, women in business and cyber security at
events around the world.
Jane has over 18 years worth of experience in cyber security and has held
directorships and senior executive positions within her own companies and
at several large PLCs, including The NCC Group. Through her latest venture,
Cyber Security Capital, she is helping to mobilise a cyber security
workforce by connecting people, opportunities and resources.

*Session 3 - Panel Event*
A panel of experts will engage with the audience and give their insights
into the challenges around attracting and retaining women in cybersecurity
roles and practical changes that organisations can take on board in terms
of removing barriers.

   - *Jacky Fox* – Director of Cyber Security and Digital Forensics

Bio as above.


   - *Jane Frankland* – Board Advisor for ClubCISO

Bio as above


   - *Orla Cox* - Director of Security Response at Symantec

Security Response is Symantec's cyber threat frontline, responsible for
identifying, analysing and responding to new attacks and threats. In her 18
years at Symantec, Orla has been at the forefront of investigations into
some of the most well-known cyber threats, such as Stuxnet and Regin. Orla
speaks regularly at industry conferences and customer events on the subject
of internet security and cyber threats. She also represents Symantec as a
media spokesperson on topical security issues.



   - *Michelle Simpson* – Security Consultant at NCC Group & OWASP Belfast
   Chapter Leader

Michelle is an ethical hacking enthusiast with 10 years of experience in IT
Security. She has a MSc. in Security & Forensic Computing from Dublin City
University, is qualified CISSP and more recently Crest Registered Tester.
Previously involved with OWASP Dublin since 2007, she co-founded the
Belfast OWASP Chapter in 2014 after moving to Belfast.



   - *Niamh Vianney Muldoon* -  Director of Information Security DocuSign

Born to Protect" best describes Niamh Vianney Muldoon. Niamh protected the
goals in her sporting career, she protects her son Crean in her personal
life and protects company data, information assets in her professional
life. Niamh has over 15 years experience in the Information Security field
across financial, online and cloud computing industries. She is known from
paving the way for young Information Technology graduates selecting to
specialize in Information Security out of college. Ireland's youngest CISSP
female and only Irish female to make finalist in UK in 2015 Women in
Technology awards. Niamh continues to give back to the Information Security
industry sitting on (ISC)2 Scholarship review panels and mentoring young
Irish Information Security Professionals.



   - *Ann Murtagh* – Ethical Hacker IBM

Ann is currently working in the security space as an Ethical Hacker in IBM.
Ann received her Masters in Security and Forensic Computing from DCU and
has recently achieved the GWAPT certification. She has worked in a number
of different roles from tech support to network engineer. Ann volunteers at
her local Coderdojo chapter in an attempt to lure the next generation into
a career in STEM. Ann is passionate about all things security and harbours
a healthy paranoia about same.
