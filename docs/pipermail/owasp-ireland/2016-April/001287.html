On 30 Mar 2016, at 19:24, Owen Pendlebury <owen.pendlebury at owasp.org<mailto:owen.pendlebury at owasp.org>> wrote:

Hello everyone,

Over the past couple of months we the Dublin board have been working on improving our reach within the community. We have noted that in order to do this the board as a group need to be more involved in promoting OWASP's mission.

To this end, two of our long serving board members Fabio Cerullo and Mark Denihan have decided to step down and allow others within the community get involved. We would like to thank them for their volunteer efforts and hope they can be still involved in some capacity for years to come.

We would like to take this opportunity to ask those interested in becoming part of the OWASP board to help drive OWASP within the local community to step forward and contact myself, Eoin Keary and Tony Clarke.

We're particularly interested in ensuring gender equality and would encourage female members of the community to get in contact and would also encourage individuals from outside pure application security such as individuals with a development or infrastructure background to become more involved.

We would also welcome others who would like to assist the OWASP Dublin chapter in other capacity with organising events or other outreach activities.

We look forward to hearing from you.
