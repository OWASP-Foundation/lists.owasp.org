> Meeting Topic: Electronic Espionage – The Expanding Threat to the US
> The incident of electronic espionage in all business, defense, and
> infrastructure sector has shown sharp
> increases. The theft of intellectual property includes the full range of
> businesses from small to large. The
> actors include criminal, Nation State, and domestic adversaries. The
> predictions are for a continued and
> growing threat. The speakers will provide case history to illustrate the
> scope of the problem including the
> threat to National Security and Criminal Cyber attacks, characterize the
> threat actors’ capabilities and
> motives, and provide guidance on what you can do to detect and defend
> against electronic espionage.
>

> Speaker Information and Bios
> 
> FBI Section Chief Peter Trahon
> Mr. Trahon is the Section Chief of the Cyber National Security Section
> (CNSS) for the Cyber Division.
> Mr. Trahon has managerial and oversight responsibilities for all FBI’s
> cyber national security
> investigations. As Section Chief for the CNSS, Mr. Trahon is also the
> Director of the National Cyber
> Investigative Joint Task Force (NCIJTF), a multi-agency federal
> taskforce collaborating to address the
> state sponsored cyber threat.
> 
> Prior to joining the FBI, Mr. Trahon designed, developed, and
> implemented software applications for
> private companies in the Boston, Massachusetts area. Mr. Trahon entered
> on duty with the FBI in 1992.
> His first assignment was in the New Orleans Division where he worked
> general economic crimes, to
> include insurance, telemarketing, and bank fraud investigations. In
> 1996, SA Trahon transferred to the
> San Francisco field office and was one of the original agents assigned
> to the International Computer
> Crime Squad. As an agent Mr. Trahon investigated several high profile
> Cyber investigations including
> CyberStrike, Solar Sunrise, Salgado, Nicholas Middelton, and Max Butler.
> In May 1999, Mr. Trahon was
> promoted to the position of Supervisory Special Agent responsible for
> the Cyber/Computer Intrusion
> squad at the San Francisco Division. During this assignment, Mr. Trahon
> managed the Division's
> Computer Analysis Response Team (CART) leading to the creation of the
> Silicon Valley Regional
> Computer Forensic Laboratory. Mr. Trahon also initiated and cultivated
> the Division's InfraGard Chapter.
> In January 2004, Mr. Trahon was promoted to Unit Chief for the
> Counterterrorism/Counterintelligence
> Criminal Intrusion Unit at FBI Headquarters. Mr. Trahon was responsible
> for overseeing the coordination
> of Federal Law Enforcement and Intelligence Community response to cyber
> threats targeting the National
> Information Infrastructure. In July 2006, Mr. Trahon was promoted to
> Assistant Special Agent in Charge
> (ASAC) for the New Haven Division. Mr. Trahon had managerial and
> oversight responsibilities for all
> Criminal and Administrative programs. In October 2008, Mr. Trahon became
> the ASAC responsible for
> Counter Terrorism, Counterintelligence, Cyber, Intelligence, Training,
> and Special Operations Programs
> for the New Haven Division. In August 2009, Mr. Trahon was promoted to
> Section Chief of the Strategic
> Outreach and Initiative Section for the Cyber Division. Mr. Trahon had
> managerial and oversight
> responsibilities for the FBI’s Online Child Exploitation Program, as
> well as, the FBI’s Cyber Training and
> InfraGard Programs.
> 
> Mr. Michael L. Kearn Vice President & Information Security Officer Wells
> Fargo Securities
> As the Information Security Officer for Interest Rate Derivatives &
> Principal Investments Group, Mike is
> responsible for proactively managing risk as it pertains to reputation,
> regulatory, and overall operational
> risk. He is also accountable for the management of privacy risk as it
> relates to technology. Mike brings
> more than eleven years of varied experience in Information Technology to
> his current role.
> Prior to joining the firm in 2005, Mike held several other positions
> with Wells Fargo & Company,
> including roles as an IT Auditor, Application Developer and Application
> Architect. Prior to joining Wells
> Fargo & Company in 2002, Mike held various roles with US Bancorp
> Institutional Trust Operations.
> Mike graduated from Hamline University in St. Paul, Minn. with a
> bachelor’s degree in International
> Economics. He is a Certified Information Systems Security Professional
> (CISSP) and holds the
> Information Assessment Methodology (IAM) certification issued by the
> United States Department of
> Defense National Security Agency (NSA).
> 
> Mike has been a featured panelist at events such as the CIO Finance
> Summit, CSO Magazine events, and
> the Minneapolis CISO Executive Summit. Mike has also provided keynote
> presentations for events such
> as the SC Magazine eSymposium on Mobile Device Security. In addition, he
> has been a featured speaker
> for the Institute for Applied Network Security (IANS) conferences and is
> a former member of their
> Technology Advisory Council (TAC). Mike is an active member of the
> Minneapolis chapter of InfraGard
> and is also the Chairman and the Cyber Security lead for the group’s
> Intelligence Committee. Mike is
> also a graduate of the FBI Citizens’ Academy and is an active member in
> the FBI Citizens’ Academy
> Alumni Association. Finally, Mike has been a featured speaker to
> government agencies, including law
> enforcement and military personnel, on various information security topics.
> 
> Mr. Jeff Hall, Director McGladrey & Pullen, LLP

> focuses on Payment Card
> Industry Reports On Compliance (ROC), network security assessments,
> financial institution general
> controls reviews and Statement on Standards for Attestation Engagements
> 16 (SSAE 16). Jeff has over

> Professional, Certified Information Security Manager, Certified in the

> Information Technology and a Payment Card Industry Qualified Security

> development and use of patent portfolios,
> licensing, due diligence studies, and infringement and validity
> investigations. He currently serves as Co-
> Chair of the firm’s Technology Committee. He also has served as an

> telecommunications industries. He currently serves on the Board of
> Directors and as Corporate Secretary
