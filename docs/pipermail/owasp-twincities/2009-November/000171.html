Call for Proposals from Speakers

Secure360°™ 2010 is shaping up as another exciting event and we would like
your help to make it an outstanding success!

The dates and location are set: May 11 & 12, 2010 at the St. Paul River
Center.

The keynote speakers will be Alan Paller, Director of Research for the SANS
Institute and Bruce Schneier, an internationally renowned security
technologist and author.

But while well known keynotes are important, hundreds of practitioners and
managers come to Secure360°™ to hear from people like YOU – experienced
professionals with specific expertise and real-world knowledge of
information risk issues and mitigation techniques.

We encourage you to submit a proposal to present an Educational Session at
Secure360°™.



Submission of Abstracts

If you have some knowledge to share and would like to submit an abstract, or
know of someone who might be interested in presenting, please visit our
website <http://www.secure360.org/user/register/speaker> and access the Call
for Presentations.

Submitters will be guided through four submission steps:

   1. Create an Account in our Speakers’ database
   2. Retrieve your personal password from your email box (Note: Registration
   emails are normally received within minutes. If you don't receive an email
   with your registration link within 12 hours, check you junk mail folder. If
   you still do not see it, contact sessions at secure360.org)
   3. Review your Bio, make any necessary changes, and add your photo (Note:
   Make sure your photo is high enough quality for both the Website and our
   printed materials.
   4. Enter up to five Session Proposals (or view the list of "My Sessions"
   that you already submitted)
   5. Submit for Review



All abstracts must be submitted by January 8, 2010!

If you have any questions please email: sessions at secure360.org

 What is Secure360°™?

Secure360°™is an annual conference providing high quality educational and
networking opportunities. It identifies developing trends in risk
management, physical security, governance, audit, information security,
contingency planning and human capital.  As the host of Secure360°™, UMSA
(Upper Midwest Security Alliance) strives to provide expert speakers,
informative educational sessions, and a wide range of exhibits and resources
for attendees.  The 2009 conference was attended by over 800 professionals.
The event offers speakers with a wide range of experience on topics such as:

ü  Physical Security

ü  IT Audit

ü  Project Management

ü  Forensics

ü  Privacy

ü  Facilities Management

ü  Incident & Emergency Response

ü  Business Continuity Management

ü  Governance

ü  Security Technology

ü  Innovation

ü  Enterprise Risk Management

To find out more about Secure360°™ view their newsletter here:
http://www.secure360.org/newsletter. <http://www.secure360.org/newsletter>
Then craft your proposal and go to this link:
http://www.secure360.org/user/register/speaker.
