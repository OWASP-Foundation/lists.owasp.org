Mission:
To find and fight the causes of insecure software.
To produce free, unbiased open source documentation, tools and standards.

Presentation:
This month Bob Sullivan presented Security Principles from the OWASP Guide
(Sam Buchanan assisted with commentary)
Hilights from discussion:
- Most of our jobs cover only a part of the scope needed to fully apply
principles like 'Fix security issues correctly'.
Often we're involved in one step of the detection,  analysis,  clean-up,  fix or
periodic audit.
To make significant change we need to educate the architects and product
managers.

- Simplicity, as a principle, battles with least priviledge and defense in
depth.

- Dealing with external systems is daunting. Sometimes these systems are within
our family of companies/colleges.
People at our organizations do not like it when we will not open up our systems
to them since, like we tell them, "we have to assume that your system has been
hacked".

Projects:
We discussed the possiblilty of presenting two sessions on Web Security at the
local ISSA conference.
The ISSA conference is May 17 & 18.
This would be an excellent way to promote Web Application security.

  Key questions:
  a. What is the audience that would attend the ISSA conference?
  b. What Web Application topics/demonstrations would be most successful?
