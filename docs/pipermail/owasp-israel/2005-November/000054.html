Hi Everybody,

This is a reminder that we at breach host the next meeting of the
Israeli ISSA chapter and since I'm going to be one of the speakers, it
is certainly going to have a lot about application security, so you
might be interested also. Please find the time to notify me if you are
coming, it is important for our preparations.

~ Ofer

-----

ISSA is the leading a not-for-profit, international organization of
information security professionals and practitioners. It provides
educational forums, publications and peer interaction opportunities that
enhance the knowledge, skill and professional growth of its members. The
Israeli Chapter of the ISSA had made it its cause to carry the flag of
information security education and knowledge in the Israel and to help
the practitioners of this field to better their skills with the most
current and advanced schools of thought.

The next meeting of the Israeli ISSA chapter will be held on November
16th, 17:30, at Breach Security <http://www.breach.com/>  offices, 11th
Bareket St. Herzliya. Participation is free and open to all, but please
inform us (mail me at ofers at breach.com) that you are coming so that we
will be better prepared. 

You can find directions for getting to Breach in the attached map.
Unfortunately we are not able to provide parking at our offices for the
visitors, but the parking lots marked in the map all have available
parking at this hour, and the closest is 12 shekels for any period of
stay.

Agenda:

17:30

Gathering, Socializing and Refreshments

17:45

ISSA Chapter Updates.
Ariel Pisetsky, Netvision, ISSA Vice-President & Co-Founder

 

18:00

Targeted attacks against web sites: would it happen to you? See for
yourself.

Ofer Shezaf, CTO, Breach Security

In recent years computer hacking has changed dramatically. Hacking is
not just for scripts kiddies that use scripts downloaded from the
Internet anymore. The most dangerous hacking is done for financial or
political gain, by organized crime, governments and terrorists. The main
difference is that modern cyber crime is targeted, effective and much
less visible.

 

The presentation will:

o               Explain the unique aspects of those targeted attacks, 

o               Bring examples of such targeted attacks from the world,

o               Include a demonstration of a targeted attack

o               Analyze whether your organization is the target of such
attacks. 

o               And lastly, discuss possible solutions.

 

19:00

Going out for drinks (for the brave ones)
