Good morning, 

 

Some of you may have encountered a scary warning sign, when trying to access
our site, warning of malware and similar unpleasantness. We’re sorry about
this, but here is what you need to know: 

 

First thing: 

The AppSecIL website has NOT been hacked, no credit cards were stolen, and
no animals harmed. 

In fact, it is literally a static, automatically generated site – if anyone
is interested in the details, we’d be happy to share. 

 

Second: 

The AppSecIL website, and all OWASP websites, do NOT host or redirect to any
type of malware, phishing pages, or other malicious websites. 

 

Our mission, as you know, is to improve the security of all websites,
everywhere. Injecting malware into our users’ machines would not serve that
purpose; nor would allowing a simple website to be attacked so easily. 

 

Third: 

This is NOT a trick test, to make sure you’re “l33t” enough to register for
a security conference ;-) 

 

So, what actually happened? 

 

Details below, but if you just want to register, you can still do this
directly on EventBrite: https://appsecil2017.eventbrite.com 

 

 

Here’s what we know so far: 

 

This is actually not a problem with our site at all, but a miscategorization
by Google, likely due to incorrectly configured intercepting VPNs. 

 

Our site uses Let’s Encrypt certificates for TLS authentication, as many
other sites do. Unfortunately many VPNs do not recognize this CA as
legitimate, so if you are behind a such corporate network that implements
decrypt-and-scan, you may have received errors earlier in the day. It is
even possible Chrome browsers encountering this would report these spoofing
attempts to Google, which would interpret this as a forged site (though this
has yet to be confirmed). 

 

Coincidentally, this was published just yesterday:
https://www.blog.google/topics/safety-security/safe-browsing-protecting-more
-3-billion-devices-worldwide-automatically/ 

In short, Google just made a big push (with AI & Machine learning) to get 3
Billion devices protected and uses machine learning 'magic' to protect the
realm. 

 

So, a site that registered a domain name, launched and got a Let's Encrypt
TLS cert all on the same day - hmmm. 

In addition the VPN issue just mentioned, with the likely browser reports,
may have been enough to push the AppSecIL site over the malicious threshold.

 

Again, some of this is just conjecture at this point, though after several
hours of investigation by OWASP staff and volunteers. Currently we have
opened a resolution request, and are waiting to hear back from Google. 

 

Bottom line: 

This was a false positive, caused by faulty crowdsourced information and
untuned machine learning, likely influenced by misconfigured networks and
questionable security products. 

Now, if only there were somewhere to hear about improving security products
and hacking machine learning… :D 

 

What should you do now? 

There is nothing for you to do about the website at this point, unless you
want to use a different browser. This will be fixed by Google at some point
in the next few hours. 

In the meantime, you can register right now! At
https://appsecil2017.eventbrite.com 

 

One other thing you can do, is talk to your network operators and system
administrators, and verify that their devices are all configured properly,
to accept Let’s Encrypt certificates and to stop breaking TLS needlessly. 

 

Again, sorry this happened, and we’re on it. 

 

Avi D

OWASP IL
