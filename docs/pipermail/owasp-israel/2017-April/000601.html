The Cloud Security Architect provides directions and leadership for all
Information security considerations within Varonis engineering, particularly
to our (Azure) SaaS solution. 

He will work closely with the Chief Architect and other security functions
(CISO) to ensure that there is coordination with their agenda, activities
and technology choices.

 

Essential Functions/Responsibilities

 

*       Lead risk assessment and penetration testing.

*       Perform gap analysis, comparing to best practices of similar
vendors, regulatory, compliance bodies and the security community at large.

*       Discuss security and compliance needs with customers.

*       Provide recommendations for security architecture, processes and
technologies and assist with their adaptation.

*       Write security related documentation. 

*       Define and prioritize security controls.

*       Educate R&D on threat landscape and security methods.

*       Perform internal and external certification audits.

 

 

Qualifications

 

*       At least 5 years in Information Security technical roles (similar
role or equivalent(

*       Previous experience with running cloud security

*       Previous experience and proven ability to define
infrastructure/systems/application architectures is preferred with In-depth
knowledge of network protocols, firewall and network architecture, secure
software design, especially under Microsoft Windows.

*       Vast Experience working with Azure AD Security, Resource management,
Scalability and Availability, Encryption and SAML\SSO configurations.

*       Proven record in leading Information Security projects from
definition to delivery

*       High level of understanding of business environment, strategy and
needs

*       Deep knowledge in Information Security Systems: Firewall/SIEM/NAC/
AV/IPS/Vulnerability assessment systems/Content filtering/DLP

*       Experience with SOC2 Type 2, ISO27001, NIST

*       familiarity with OWASP standards

*       Excellent English (both speaking and writing(
