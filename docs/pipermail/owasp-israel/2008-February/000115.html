BTW, the "locked down" solution can be easily used by an attacked to DOS
a specific user... 
Let's say that the victim is supposed to perform some operation in the
site, and the attacker knows about it. All he has to do is intentionally
fail to authenticate (just before the victim) under the victim's account
the number of times it needs to block him from the login.

number of possible passwords and by that weakened the security of the
total solution.
