Topic - CRM Security

Abstract

Customer relationship management (CRM) systems are increasingly being used
by organizations to increase revenue and improve efficiencies within their
sales organizations. The overall objective of CRM is to identify, acquire,
and retain customers, meaning that these systems contain information that
has a direct impact on the competitive advantage of the organizaiton. It is
therefore critical that this information be protected from unauthorized
access or modification. This session will highlight the risks inherent
within these systems and how to address them from both a process and
technical perspective.

Speaker:

Mark Adams

Mark is a subject matter expert in the area of IT security, compliance and
audit. He has almost seven years of experience working for “Big 4” public
accounting firms where he specialized in reviews of information system
security, as well as compliance with regulations such as Sarbanes-Oxley,
HIPAA, and GLBA. Prior to his Big 4 career he supported IT departments in
both the banking and energy sectors for over six years as a network,
systems, and security administrator. His work for clients has included risk
management consulting, penetration testing, IT controls evaluation, and
security policy reviews and training. He currently works in the Application
Security Group at Halliburton where he works with project managers in making
sure that their applications can pass an audit.
