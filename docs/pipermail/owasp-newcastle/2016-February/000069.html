Hey Folks,

I just wanted to point out a few cool things that have changed on the wiki
in the past few weeks.

Xiaoran Wang just updated the XXE page in a way that (I think) finally
brings it up to snuff. Defending against XXE has been illusive and many
defensive guides were just wrong. I think this is solid now. Thanks
Xiaoran!
https://www.owasp.org/index.php/XML_External_Entity_(XXE)_Processing

Abashkin Anton just dropped a mass assignment cheat sheet on us. If you
have time to review, please do so!
https://www.owasp.org/index.php?title=Mass_Assignment_Cheat_Sheet

Tasha Carl has been prolific trying to clean up many of the language
specific areas of the wiki. In particular, Tasha has been working on
cleaning up the Java category and it's now a lot more updated and relevant.
https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Category:Java

Shruti Kulkarni has also been prolific in dropping good content on the
foundation. Shruti has been making cheatsheet updates such as the access
control https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Access_Control_Cheat_Sheet and
other cheatsheets, and just provided a threat model guide that I'm
reviewing before we wikifi it. Thank you Shruti!

There are many others who continue to work on the wiki, please forgive me
for not mentioning your work. Thanks all!

Aloha,
Jim
