Hi all,

I am delighted to announce the final agenda for the OWASP New Zealand
Day 2009. Two more talks have been selected since the first speakers
announcement.

We are going to have a key note:

- Insecurity and the Internet, Paul Craig - Security-Assessment.com

And we have 6 talks stuffed with high quality topics and great speakers:

- Vulnerabilities in Action, Brett Moore - Insomnia Security
- Testing Web Services, Nick von Dadelszen – Lateral Security
- Exploiting Firefox Extensions, Roberto Suggi Liverani / Nick Freeman
- Security-Assessment.com
- Application Bug Chaining, Mark Piper - Catalyst IT Ltd
- "Where Worlds Collide" - PCI-DSS for OWASP practioners, Dean Carter
- Security-Assessment.com
- XSS – The Gloves are Off, Andy Prow / Kirk Jackson - Aura Software
Security / Xero

Final agenda is available at:
http://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_New_Zealand_Day_2009#tab=Agenda

REMINDER:

Online registration will remain open till the 6th July. If you haven't
registered yet, please do so by visiting the following link:

http://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_New_Zealand_Day_2009

For those who are already registered, please confirm your attendance
by simply replying me before the 6th July as morning and afternoon tea
will be offered by the sponsors and we need to confirm number of
attendees. Entry to the event is free, please confirm your attendance.

Finally, a big thanks to the sponsors (Security-Assessment.com and
Lateral Security), to the speakers and to the conference committee:
Lech Janczewski , Alexandre Medarov and Rob Munro for making this
conference a great success.

See you all the 13th July,

Thanks,

Roberto Suggi Liverani
OWASP NZ Leader
