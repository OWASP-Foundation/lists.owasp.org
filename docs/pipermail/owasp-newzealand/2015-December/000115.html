HI All,



We have decided to extend the deadline for the OWASP New Zealand Day 2016
CFP and CFT until the 22nd of December. If you have a topic you'd like to
present on or training you'd like to give, please fill in the table at
https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_New_Zealand_Day_2016#Call_For_Presentations
and email to adrian.hayes at owasp.org, denis.andzakovic at owasp.org and
kim.carter at owasp.org.

On Mon, Sep 14, 2015 at 3:31 PM, Kim Carter <kim.carter at owasp.org> wrote:

> Hi Everyone,
>
>
> Today we are proud to announce the seventh OWASP New Zealand Day
> conference, to be held at the University of Auckland on Thursday February
> 4th, 2016. The call for presenters, trainers, and sponsors is now open.
> We will open registration for general attendees in the coming weeks, so
> stay tuned as registrations will be limited.
>
> OWASP New Zealand Day is a free, one-day conference dedicated to
> application security with an emphasis on secure development techniques
> and management to help Kiwis build more secure applications.
>
> Similarly to last year:
>
> * We will be offering training on the day before the conference
> (Wednesday Feb 3th) similar to previous years.
> * After lunch on the conference day, we hope to split into two tracks -
> one focused on deep technical topics, the other on policy, compliance
> and risk management.
>
>
> We have a large amount of presenter slots that we need to fill, on
> everything from deeply technical topics to risk and compliance.
> Presentation submissions are welcome from all interested parties, we're
> especially interested in hearing from the developer and other technical
> communities, not just security professionals. Keep in mind we will not
> consider vendor pitches or commercial content. More information is
> available here:
>
> https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_New_Zealand_Day_2016#Call_For_Presentations
>
> We also have a number of training slots we'd like to fill. If you have a
> security related training you'd like to give the day before the main
> conference, please make a submission. More information is available
> here:
>
> https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_New_Zealand_Day_2016#Call_For_Trainers
>
> OWASP is strictly not for profit and to keep our conference free we need
> sponsors to support us. The sponsorship money is used to help make OWASP
> New Zealand Day 2016 a free, compelling, and valuable experience for the
> audience. More information about sponsorship is available here:
>
> https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_New_Zealand_Day_2016#Call_For_Sponsorships
