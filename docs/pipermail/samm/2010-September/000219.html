i agree 100%; "test it out" is both a business and a nonsense for all... but
management.
Sure it is highly repeatable; and further, i'm pretty sure that there is
more business to do with "build in" than with "test out"; but, for
convincing stakeholders or top management, testing output is much more
handleable.
i saw many IT projects chose keeping applications at risk, while conducting
assessments for leveraging budget the next year, by showing off inherent
risk to managers, risk and business owners.
Learning curve seems mandatory, and we need base figures, and that's what i
really miss actually..
Likewise, ROI calculators have been given last week by IBM or HP for their
"test out" products, but do we have kind of ROI for "built in"
tools/methodologies?
For instance, very few companies implement security from design phase;
likewise, solution providers miss it too: Microsoft's Threat Modeling tool
seems alone in these earlier phases?
basically, i find SAMM powerful for planning or proving.
It seems to me that the main purpose today is to prove;
Appsec therefore seems, from my point of view, much more often driven by
"verification", than by "construction" activities..?
