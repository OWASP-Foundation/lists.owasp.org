On Wed, Apr 7, 2010 at 8:58 AM, Rohit Sethi <rklists at gmail.com> wrote:

> That's a good question - I haven't really mapped the controls back to
> SAMM, and that has to do with the points you raised in your second
> paragraph.
>
> Some of the controls, specifically around aligning security activities
> to business, are more about strategic alignment and cost control than
> necessarily reducing the total quantity of vulnerabilities. External
> auditors are really interested in risk reduction.
>
> I'm not so sure that means that an external auditor only cares about
> verification / deployment activities. I know that if an outsourced
> vendor includes secure requirements/design/architecture then there
> will be fewer inherent vulnerabilities in the application itself and
> less time spent on remediation. Good governance & education will also
> likely mean fewer vulnerabilities which decreases the risk of
> vulnerabilities slipping through the verification steps.
>
> My take is that it should be the auditor's discretion to take the
> results of the audit and provide an overall "health" rating. When we
> used this audit framework, we did provide the overall SAMM maturity
> ratings for what we assessed. People are probably more concerned with
> how they compare to other organizations in their industry.
>
>
