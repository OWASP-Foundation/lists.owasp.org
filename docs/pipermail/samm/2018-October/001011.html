Hello Guillermo

This is the old scoring system at V 1.0, I suggest you use SAMM version
1.5 scoring. This will give you a 1.3 or 0.7 score rather than 1+ or 0+.
since the possible values are still 0 to 3, you can translate that to a
1-10 scale without problem.

Regards,
Juan C Calderon

On 10/4/2018 10:52 AM, Guillermo Caminer wrote:
> Hello list!
> I've used openSAMM for evaluate the state of security practices in
> several clients. One thing that managers/directors don't like, is the
> scoring scale, which usually is between 0 and 1+. Even after
> explaining that 0+ or even 1 is not necessarily bad, they just don't
> like the near to zero resulting numbers.
> This may seem silly to us (because we know the model in depth), but
> for showing executive reports to management, I've found that the scale
> could be different. I suggest using an exponential scale (from 1 to
> 10). Because the current lineal scale does not express realistically
> the disminution in risk, that is, the number 2 in a security practice
> is not twice that 1, both in effort to achieve 2 and in risk reduction
> (2 is much bigger than the double of 1).
>
> Just my thoughts.
