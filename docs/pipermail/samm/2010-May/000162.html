When I applied SAMM to my organization, I did find the Operational Enablement practice area hard to follow but I was new to the model and finding my way at the time. I understood Operational Enablement to address documentation and procedures specific to the application and how it is configured and how it needs to be run and maintained. 

As you point out, why is "code signing" a level 3 Operational Enablement
 activity - shouldn't "code signing" be considered under Environment 
Hardening rather than Operational Enablement? 

Your question about event logging is also good. There is a requirement to "Document procedures for typical application alerts" as a level 1 OE activity, but there should be more within Operational Enablement to ensure proper log management and archival, correlation and reviews, forensics and so on. 

The tie-ins between Operational Enablement, Environment Hardening and Vulnerability Management need to be reviewed and clarified. I think the "Solid Incident Response Capabilities" which you are looking for in Operational Enablement is addressed under the Vulnerability Management practice, not Operational Enablement, for example. 

/Jim Bird
