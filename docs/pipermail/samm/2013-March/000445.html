At the risk of repeating my previous mail to the list on this subject,
my view is that some of this content is required for relevance. 
Presently, I don't see much that does make OpenSAMM relevant.  It would
be nice for that to change so that when one advocates the use of a
generalist and non-vendor attached maturity model, there is something to
work with besides whatever controls or process people spin up in a local
brainstorming session; a generally non-repeatable, non-standard, and
likely difficult to demonstrate proper governance should it be needed.

People want to split specific QA/maturity methods away from generalized
concept model in a wiki?  Fine. 

People want to combine them?  Sure. 

Just please make it relevant to people attempting to stand up a client
program or improve an existing one.  The other option is continued
irrelevance and defacto endorsement of a lock-in vendor workflow which
may or may not benefit anyone except the vendor who has used it to sell
their compliance product.

Remember how we became a compliance industry instead of a security industry.
