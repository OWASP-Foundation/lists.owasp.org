Thanks for the comments, Ben.

We have been using CSF but it has a number of issues: (1) As you said, it
doesn't address the secure coding/product security side of things, (2) It's
not designed to be a maturity model and there are major gaps (although we
had adapted it to some degree), and (3) It does not align well with either
BSIMM and SAMM (My opinion, of course, but I tried!) Since we have so many
products and a robust security program in general, the model needs to have
a cohesive structure and scoring methodology across the enterprise and
product domains. We weren't happy with the results when we tried to
"feather " SAMM into CSF, or into any model for that matter. And, we
thought it would make analysis a nightmare.

Even though C2M2 was generated for the Energy sector, I have found the
domains/objectives/practices to be very comprehensive and generic enough to
be adapted to general cybersecurity use. (It also looks like the DHS Cyber
Resilience Review might be based on C2M2.) I did evaluate numerous models
and we are not using C2M2 "out of the box;" we have customized to fit our
needs and the scope of our program. However, I think it is working really
well. So, kudos! In fact, we are working on a Data Privacy initiative and I
took a quick stab at incorporating a Data Privacy Program domain. I was
very happy with the draft that came out of that.

Thanks again.

Kim

> too). Unfortunately, CSF is almost completely silent on appsec/sw
> assurance and isn't perhaps as good a match.
>
> Anyway, fwiw, tldr; is that you should be able to stick SAMM into

> align scoring.
