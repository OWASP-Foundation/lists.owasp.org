>     One thing to bear in mind with BSIMM and SAMM is that they weren't
>     really conceived as CMMs or anything like that. BSIMM, for example, was
>     purely observational and arbitrary. Cigital went to their top clients
>     and evaluated what they (Cigital) thought made their clients appsec
>     programs "good" and then collected those criteria iteratively over
>     time.
>     SAMM was originally created by the same person who did most of the
>     BSIMM
>     work, and it's since grown and evolved from there, but one must
>     remember
>     that it's as much a collection of "best practices" (hate that
>     phrase) as
>     it is anything else; and, in many ways the "maturity" aspect of it is
>     oriented toward identifying and planning for dependencies and sequences
>     of practice evolution over time.
> 
>     Where SAMM and all these other CMMs breakdown is in the measurement of
>     "presence of practice" vs "effectiveness of practice." It's a difficult
>     thing to do well. For example, just because code is being scanned by
>     DAST or SAST or whatever does not mean that those practices are
>     *effective* within the program. And, moreover, the definition of
>     "effective" will vary between orgs depending on how they function
>     (e.g.,
>     waterfall vs agile vs DevOps vs an unholy mix of the three).
> 
>     I understand the drivers for using these models, frameworks, etc., as a
>     vendor, because it gives you something to point at. However, one
>     must be
>     careful to use them as an input into a program and not to let them
>     become the obsessive focus of the program. Too often I see executive
>     demands to move to the next maturity level that devolve into
>     box-checking and tool-deploying without actually measuring
>     effectiveness, which is at the heart of the original CMM.
>     Unfortunately,
>     too much short-cutting occurred with it back in the day, and we see
>     similar pitfalls with all these approaches.
> 
>     As for CSF...I find it most disappointing that they've completely left
>     out software security/assurance. I've yet to get a good answer on why
>     they did that and did not resolve it in v1.1. :/
>
