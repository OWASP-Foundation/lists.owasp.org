Hi Mike,

thanks for your answer (I saw it in the digest).

> Interesting question. As far as I know: NOT.
>
> One of the strengths imho of OWASP and this great SAMM project is that
it is open for all.  So open to:
> - use and  improve without borders.

> I think that companies that have implemented SAMM (with or without help)
> should receive some kind of recognition. But not the ISO-way, but far
> more open and inline with OWASP philosophy. Maybe something like an open
> badge certification is something considering for the next-release. With
> this I mean:
>
> 1) something companies can show that they have implemented SAMM (e.g.
> logo on site , see e.g. how the CII program has handled this:
> https://bestpractices.coreinfrastructure.org/<https://bestpractices.coreinfrastructure.org/> ) This gives credit to the
> company and their customers.
>
> 2) Some open transparent defined process proves that companies showing
> the badge really earn the badge (or SAMM approved logo -). This can also
> give SAMM and companies using it a boost.

I like this proposal, I agree the ISO way is not the best for SAMM, but it would certainly be good to have both of the above.  It's a nice way of being able to prove one has undertaken the steps to implement SAMM.

To ask a different question - has anyone used SAMM as a basis for gaining another accreditation/certification?

The reason behind this is to give some badge/proof to customers that we create cyber secure solutions, and have followed a well defined model in doing so.
