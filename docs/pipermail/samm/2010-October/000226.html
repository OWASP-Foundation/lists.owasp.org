On Tue, Oct 5, 2010 at 12:31 AM, Jeff Williams
<jeff.williams at aspectsecurity.com> wrote:
>> The processes above are measuring the inherent risk....
>> ... and the application of these controls adjust the inherent risk to produce the residual risk.
>
> Well, that is the traditional way to view the problem.  And it leads exactly to the situation most companies are in -- continuous vulnerability chasing.

ASVS recommends a specific TOV based on the measurement of inherent risk.

Furthermore,  http://www.owasp.org/index.php/Category:OWASP_Top_Ten_Project
2010 Release was promoted as "... managing risk, not just avoiding
vulnerabilities" and "To manage these risks, organizations need an
application risk management program ..."

The contention is the reference to "risk management" above and that
"risk management" is practiced in companies regardless of their level
of maturity.

To quote http://www.owasp.org/index.php/Category:OWASP_Top_Ten_Project
again "“we’ll never hack our way secure – it’s going to take a culture
change” for organizations to properly address application security"
i.e. "culture change" is the same as increasing the maturity.

>> Subsequently, an immature development culture will resist a controls
>> based approach i.e. ASVS, ESAPI which is the similar to the resistance
>> to both ISO 27001 and PCI i.e. controls based.
>
> There's a difference between compliance and providing actual security mechanisms. ESAPI is succeeding because it makes application security easier - training, rqmts, architecture, implementation, verification, and remediation.  I see no resistance to ESAPI in organization - even immature ones - in fact quite the opposite.

I know of someone in Australia whose implementations of ISO 27001 are
based on leveraging the COBIT Maturity Model and CMMI to create a ITIL
governance framework based on SLA.

Outside of Aspect Security i.e.
http://www.owasp.org/index.php/Category:OWASP_Enterprise_Security_API#tab=Here.27s_what_I_did_with_ESAPI
- are their any reference implementations of production software being
shipped leveraging ESAPI that you could provide?

I believe that the use of replacement API which provide some form of
security should be considered an activity of both OpenSAMM and BSIMM
but they might already be address by an existing activity - thoughts?
