>> From my reading of the Domains and Practices (excluding Activities) of
> the Software Security Framework specified by BSIMM1/2 I have concluded
> that the following correlation may (i.e. this could be incorrect)
> exist with OpenSAMM v1.0 (excluding Objectives):
> 
> Within the “Governance” Domain of BSIMM:
> - The “Compliance and Policy” Practice of BSIMM is listed in reverse
> within OpenSAMM i.e. “Policy and Compliance” Security Practice and;
> - The “Training” Practice of BSIMM is listed as the “Education and
> Guidance” Security Practice of OpenSAMM.
> 
> The "Intelligence" Domain of BSIMM is the "Construction" Business
> Function of OpenSAMM:
> - The “Attack Models” Practice within BSIMM is “Threat Assessment”
> Security Practice of OpenSAMM and;
> - “Security Features and Design” Practice within BSIMM is “Security
> Requirements” Security Practice of OpenSAMM and;
> - “Standards and Requirements” Practice within BSIMM  is “Secure
> Architecture” Security Practice of OpenSAMM.
> 
> The “SSDL Touchpoints” Domain of BSIMM is the “Verification” Business
> Function of OpenSAMM:
> - The “Architecture Analysis” Practice of BSIMM is “Design Review”
> Security Practice of OpenSAMM.
> 
> Within the "Deployment" Domain of BSIMM:
> - The “Penetration Testing” Practice of BSIMM is “Vulnerability
> Management” Security Practice of OpenSAMM and;
> - The “Software Environment” Practice of BSIMM is “Environment
> Hardening” Security Practice of OpenSAMM and;
> - The “Configuration Management and Vulnerability Management” Practice
> of BSIMM is addressed in part by “Operational Enablement” Security
> Practice of OpenSAMM
> 
> 
> While I intent to present the possible correlation with Activities of
> BSIMM to the Objectives of OpenSAMM shortly I believe that correlation
> at the (BSIMM) Domain/ (OpenSAMM) Business Function and (BSIMM)
> Practice/(OpenSAMM) Security Practice first will align and correct any
> possible errors above.
