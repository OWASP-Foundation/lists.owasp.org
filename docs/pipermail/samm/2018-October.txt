Hello list!
I've used openSAMM for evaluate the state of security practices in several
clients. One thing that managers/directors don't like, is the scoring
scale, which usually is between 0 and 1+. Even after explaining that 0+ or
even 1 is not necessarily bad, they just don't like the near to zero
resulting numbers.
This may seem silly to us (because we know the model in depth), but for
showing executive reports to management, I've found that the scale could be
different. I suggest using an exponential scale (from 1 to 10). Because the
current lineal scale does not express realistically the disminution in
risk, that is, the number 2 in a security practice is not twice that 1,
both in effort to achieve 2 and in risk reduction (2 is much bigger than
the double of 1).

Just my thoughts.

Best regards!
