Thanks for your comments, Christian!

I agree with Seba's response for the most part. The Quickstart guide is
really an exercise in cutting things out - what's the least amount of
detail we can cover, but still give a person a clear understanding of what
SAMM is all about?

Even so, I think I'm missing part of your comment, which could make the
Quickstart guide better if I understood better:

You mentioned "the Lightweight approach of OpenSAMM" again.
Do you mean that SAMM is "lightweight" in comparison to other standards, or
that there is a defined way to implement SAMM in a "lightweight" manner?

If there is a defined "lightweight approach" *for* SAMM, then I'm not
familiar with it. Could you give a link or two to get me started?

If we're comparing to other standards, then I think there may be a place
for it in the Quickstart. For people who work with those other standards,
it would be helpful here to say just a few words about how they relate. I'm
imagining a single paragraph that covers the most significant "other
standards", without distracting people who don't already know those other
standards.

I could use some help with that. Is there anyone on the list who can touch
on the differences & similarities with the other relevant standards in just
a few words?  I'll try to fit it in and we can all see what it looks like
then.
