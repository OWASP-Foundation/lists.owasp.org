Hey Joe.

As Colin & Christian mentioned, BSIMM is a periodically updates survey that captures what companies say they're doing about software security. My general rule of thumb was to use OpenSAMM for organizations that were fairly immature and/or jut getting started with software security. For organizations that were more sophisticated (say maybe a year or two in to a software security program), BSIMM provided some rough data to measure the organization against. 

For a while I was really tempted to try to produce similar types of BSIMM stats against SAMM, but I stopped since I consider it harmful after seeing a few pathological organizations that are striving to hit BSIMM averages (because of know-nothing execs blindly chasing metrics) as opposed to practicing good risk management and making intelligent trade-off decisions that are relevant to the specifics of their organization. SAMM clearly sides with the latter group... You know your org, you make the calls.

p.
