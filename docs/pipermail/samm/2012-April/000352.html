I'll just leave this thought here though -

I use SAMM often to bench-mark an org; and what would really be useful to
me is if we could turn it into a tool; that people could use to self-audit
and for the 'stats' to update in real time. In this way the document would
be living; but also it could contain historical measurements as well;
allowing one to see their progress or impact over-time; and also measure
trajectory toward their goals. I imagine a kind of web-app for this - but
do not know what it would look like. Is anybody else interested in such a
tool?

> Hey Joe.
>
> As Colin & Christian mentioned, BSIMM is a periodically updates survey
> that captures what companies say they're doing about software security. My
> general rule of thumb was to use OpenSAMM for organizations that were
> fairly immature and/or jut getting started with software security. For
> organizations that were more sophisticated (say maybe a year or two in to a
> software security program), BSIMM provided some rough data to measure the
> organization against.
>
> For a while I was really tempted to try to produce similar types of BSIMM
> stats against SAMM, but I stopped since I consider it harmful after seeing
> a few pathological organizations that are striving to hit BSIMM averages
> (because of know-nothing execs blindly chasing metrics) as opposed to
> practicing good risk management and making intelligent trade-off decisions
> that are relevant to the specifics of their organization. SAMM clearly
> sides with the latter group... You know your org, you make the calls.
>
