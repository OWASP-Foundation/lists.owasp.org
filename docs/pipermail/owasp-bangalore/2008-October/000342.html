Considering both aspects, I think Security professionals will have more
brain work to do. Due to the reducing costs, organizations may look out for
intelligent solutions with reduced cost. I am very optimistic that the
security aspect in the software will not be left our just because of
monitory reasons because, this is more dangerous. Smart solutions at reduced
cost will be the in-thing in my opinion.

On Sat, Oct 11, 2008 at 1:51 PM, Rajiv Vishwa <rajivvishwa at gmail.com> wrote:

> Hi All,
>
> Just was curious to bring the topic "Recession Vs Enterprise Security"
> topic to limelight. This has been discussed a lot in various other forums
> now-a-days but wanted to know what you guys have to say about this.
>
> There are two possible consequences of global recession.
>
> 1. Management will start thinking that security is an add-on for existing
> business, considered as an additional expense than investments/ future
> benefits. This will reduce the importance of security guys around.
>
> 2. Hackers understand that security measures are relaxed due to budget
> constrains, which will make their lives easier. More and more hackers
> evolve. This implies opportunity for people in security domain and keeps
> them busy.
>
> These two possibilities are absolutely contradictory when we consider the
> impact of recession on security domain.
>
> So what do you guys say. Which is most probable to happen 1 or 2 ???
