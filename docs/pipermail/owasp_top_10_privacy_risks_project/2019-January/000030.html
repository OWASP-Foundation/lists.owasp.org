On 19/12/2018 10:05, Florian Stahl wrote:
>
> Dear Privacy Pros,
>
> time is flying and in September 2019 it will be 5 years that the OWASP 
> Top 10 Privacy Risks have been published. In 2014 we pointed out 
> privacy risks that today are more relevant than ever from my point of 
> view.
>
> Anyway after 5 years it is time to review and potentially update the 
> list and see if GDPR changed the privacy risk landscape. So I am 
> looking for volunteers to participate.
>
> The method we used in 2014 can be reused to derive the OWASP Top 10 
> Privacy Risks 2019. Thus, the effort should be less than the first 
> time and the preliminary timeline is the following:
>
>   * 31 January 2019: Core Team Established
>
>   * 31 March 2019: Assessment Method Reviewed & Optimized
>
>   * 15 June 2019: Candidate Risks for the Top 10 list identified
>
>   * 31 October 2019: Risks (impact and likelihood) assessed and Top 10
>     Privacy Risks 2019 published
>
> Let me know until 31 January if you want to be part of the core team 
> and help to keep this popular project up-to-date and share your 
> valuable experiences with others.
>
> Of course also feedback to the proposed approach is welcome.
>
> Kind regards and happy holidays,
>
> Florian
