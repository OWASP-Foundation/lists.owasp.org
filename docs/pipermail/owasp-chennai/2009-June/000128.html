Shanmuga

One of your points is alright. FTP Credentials will have to be changed now,
with this worm out in the wild. Default settings on FTP can be compromised
anyway, worm or no worm. The worm does not try and bruteforce your FTP
credentials. It merely gleans the passwords and reports it to the bot herder

Unfortunately this worm does not stop at FTP credentials. It even disables
AV and patching and goes a step further to install its own bogus AV
solution. Keep an eye out for any bogus AV apps you may find in your
network. This is a hard worm to detect because of its obfuscated nature.

With reference to your point on the Chinese IPs, there were several more IPs
that were found distributing the worm (in different countries) like Russia,
etc. Censorship of the IP/IP range is not a solution. These worms are
largely driven from proxy sites and fake dynamic dns', which can be created
for free. These worms have a distributed set of botnets working in tandem.

I would pay more attention Traffic analysis, AV Signatures, Patching, IPS
sigs and browser security. Enterprise browser security is in the middle ages
even today. Even today, we have IE6, which are being driven from Active
Directories. Yesterday, while on assessment, we discovered some affected
systems that were sending out a boatload of syns to some random IPs on the
internet.

You can get some more info at my blog citadelnotes.blogspot.com
