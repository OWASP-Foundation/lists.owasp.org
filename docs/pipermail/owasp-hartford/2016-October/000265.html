In comparing/contrasting SAMM vs BSIMM, you will conclude:

 

-        SAMM is open and community driven. BSIMM is driven by a single
vendor

-        SAMM is better in addressing the qualitative aspects of application
security and connected to the OWASP body of knowledge whereas BSIMM is more
quantitative and connected to the consulting model of the particular vendor
that drives it

-        For organizations that are starting off, they may need prescriptive
guidance which is anti-agile. For organizations that are capable of doing
own understanding then SAMM is available at a lower total cost.

The BSIMM is a measuring stick for software security. The best way to use
the BSIMM is to compare and contrast your own initiative with the data about
what other organizations are doing contained in the model. You can then
identify goals and objectives of your own and refer to the BSIMM to
determine which additional activities make sense for you.

 

The BSIMM data show that high maturity initiatives are well-rounded-carrying
out numerous activities in all 12 of the practices described by the model.
The model also describes how mature software security initiatives evolve,
change, and improve over time.
