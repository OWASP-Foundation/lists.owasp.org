Dear CISO colleague,

as a respected information security leader in the industry, OWASP (Open
Web Application Security Project, www.owasp.org <http://www.owasp.org>)
would like to hear your opinion!

Link to take the CISO Survey 2014 now
<https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/CISOSurvey2014>

OWASP is preparing the Global CISO report 2014 and conducting a survey
among CISOs and senior information security managers in relation to
application security with the aim of providing you with new insights
about the state of application security across various industry sectors
and about new security trends and aligning our efforts to better help
solving the problems of the future that you face.

The survey shall take only a few minutes of your precious time and by
completing it you are helping shape the future of Internet and software
security. At the conclusion of the survey, the aggregated results will
be publicly available in the form of a free report on the owasp.org
website, keeping your information completely anonymous. (If you are
interested, the published results of the last CISO Survey Report 2013
can be found https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_CISO_Survey.)

As you may know, OWASP is a volunteer open-source organization dedicated
to fighting the causes of software insecurity. We are also a registered
charity & non-profit in the USA and the EU. See more at
https://www.owasp.org/index.php/About_OWASP

The survey can be found here:
*https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/CISOSurvey2014*

Or if you prefer a different language, this survey is also available in:
** Chinese: **https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/CISOSurvey2014cn**
*** Hebrew: **https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/CISOSurvey2014iw**
*** Japanese: **https://jp.surveymonkey.com/s/CISOSurvey2014jp*

*Early participants, **before September-1**5* (23:59 GMT), *can take
part in a raffle*. If you provide your contact details at the end of the
survey, you will be entered into a drawing for one of the following
prizes, generously donated by EC-Council and OWASP:

* 1 Free conference ticket for? AppSecUSA
<http://2014.appsecusa.org/2014/> (September 18-19 in Denver) (lucky
draw on Sep-11)
* 1 free OWASP training for security managers at the AppSec USA 2014
conference "Managing Web & Application Security ??? OWASP for Senior
Managers"
<http://2014.appsecusa.org/2014/training/managing-web-application-security-owasp-for-senior-managers>
(lucky draw on Sep-11)
* 1 free ticket for the CISO Asia Summit & Roundtable
<http://www.ciso-summit.com/ciso-asia.html>* 1 CCISO Training and Exam
Package from EC-Council

Thank you very much in advance for your time and input.

The Summit Schedule is now on-line including this years OWASP track.   
https://www.rochestersecurity.org
Also, We have added 4 In-Depth Training sessions to this year?s October
7-8 event.
 
Dennis Allen and others from CERT will conduct a hands-on session titled
?Hands-on Threat Detection and Analysis?.This session will be offered twice.
Also, we have software security training with Jim Manico. Jim is an
author and educator of developer security awareness training and has a
17 year history building software as a developer and architect.
 
Jim will host 2 different sessions:
 
The first session covers: HTTP Basics and Introduction, Cross-site
Scripting and HTML Hacking, Cross Site Request Forgery, Clickjacking,
SQL and other Injection
 
The second covers: HTML5 Security Considerations, App Layer Intrusion
Detection, Mobile Security
Webservice Security, Applied SSL
 
Detailed descriptions of these sessions are available at:
https://www.rochestersecurity.org/schedule/in-depth-training/
 
Due to limited seating for these in-depth training sessions, session
registration is requires in addition to conference registration.
Registration is free for paid attendees of the summit. Register for
these sessions as well as the summit at:
https://www.rochestersecurity.org/schedule/in-depth-training/
