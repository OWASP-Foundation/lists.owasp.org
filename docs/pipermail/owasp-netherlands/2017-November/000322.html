Got your tickets yet? Don't be to late!

The OWASP BeNeLux Day 2017 will take place on 23 and 24 November 2017, in
Tilburg (The Netherlands). On Thursday 23 November, three 1-day
trainings are offered (in parallel):
    •    WebGoat - Teaching application security 101 (by Nanne Baars)
    •    Whiteboard Hacking aka Hands-on Threat Modeling (by Sebastien
Deleersnyder)
    •    Secure Development: Models and best practices (by Bart De Win)

Social event, Thursday 23 November, starting at 7PM
    •    Dudok Tilburg: http://www.dudok.nl/

On Friday 24 November, the 1-day conference features a line-up of various
regional and international speakers:
    •    Attribute Based Access Control. Why, what, how? (by Jacoba
Sieders)
    •    How to spend $3.6mil on one coding mistake, and other fun stuff
you can do with $3.6mil (by Matias Madou)
    •    The evil friend in your browser (by Achim D. Brucker)
    •    Exploring the ecosystem of malicious domain registrations in the
.eu TLD (by Lieven Desmet)
    •    Don't trust the DOM: Bypassing XSS mitigations via script gadgets
(by Sebastian Lekies)
    •    A Series of Unfortunate Events: Where Malware Meets Murphy (by
Mattijs van Ommeren)
    •    Common REST API security pitfalls (by Philippe De Ryck)
    •    Creating An AppSec Pipeline With Containers In A Week How We
Failed And Succeeded (by Jeroen Willemsen)

Attendance to the conference is free of charge, but registration is
required: https://owasp-benelux-day-2017.eventbrite.com/
If you register for a training, please consider to become an OWASP member.

We have a limited number of seats available, so first come first serve.
More information on the program and venue can be found here.
