Following from Christian's lead...
Hi everyone, my name is David, and its been 3 hours since I last tested the
security of a web application.

I have been aware of OWASP for a number of years now.  My interest in the
organisation is primarily from the security testing perspective.  I don't
make any claim to skills in software development, but I have been doing
security assessment in one form or another for around 12 years.

I discussed the idea of starting a local OWASP chapter in Perth with several
people over the last maybe year or two.  However, when I finally got round
to looking into it I discovered, to my surprise, that I'd been beaten to the
punch by Josh Qwek.  (Nice work Josh)

Anyway, now that the deed has been done, I am really keen to help arrange
meetings on some sort of semi-regular basis.  I have spoken to Peter Wolski
from AISA and he has suggested that we could hold an inaugural OWASP meeting
as a joint OWASP / AISA event at the beginning of next year.  This would
take care of venue (E&Y office) and sponsorship / refreshments for the first
meeting.  After this, we can make our own plans.

How does this sound to everybody?

Regards,
Dave Taylor
