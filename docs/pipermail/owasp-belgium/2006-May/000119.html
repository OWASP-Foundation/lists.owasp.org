Dear Security Professional, 

The ISSA Brussels-European Chapter is proud to announce its next
Security Event entitled:

"Where the Security Triad meets the Legal Triad." 

This event is not to be considered like a clash between Chinese gangs
(see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triads
<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triads> ) but rather like a marriage of
understanding with complementary partners, as we'll see : 

The Legal Triad of Law, Regulation and Contracts can fit seamlessly with
the Security Triad of Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability. In
the first presentation this will be explained in general whereas the
second presentation will address one specific subtopic, software escrow.

SPEAKERS' BIO'S AND ABSTRACTS OF THE PRESENTATIONS :

Jan Leysen

Jan Leysen is lawyer in Kortrijk since 1986. Mediator in Commercial Law
since 2004. Partner LEYSEN-VANDELANOTTE-DEVLOO LAWOFFICE KORTRIJK.
Suppliant Judge High Court Kortrijk. 

He co-authored 2 books on IT: 'Informatiseren in de KMO: managements- en
juridische aspecten' (Mys & Breesch, 2000) and 'Informatiebeveiliging
binnen locale besturen' (V-ICT-OR, 2005). 

abstract : 

For every part of the Security Triad there is a corresponding part with
regards to the Law, Regulations and Contracts, which are the
cornerstones of the legal profession.
This presentation will give you another view on IT & IT Security than
the one you're oh so familiar with. 

Maurice Gruyaert

Ir. Maurice Gruyaert is Managing Director of Escrow Europe NV. 

Maurice has been working in the ICT market for more than 30 years.
Before joining Escrow Europe NV he worked for companies like
Siemens-Nixdorf, Digital Equipment (Compaq, HP), Xerox,
PriceWaterhouseCoopers, De Federale Verzekeringen,...  

He has engineering degrees in Electronics, Nuclear Physics and
Electro-Mechanics 

abstract : 

Many organisations are dependant in their day-to-day activities on third
party technology. Dependency implies RISK. It is crucial for technology
users to minimize their expose to risks. An escrow arrangement is the
common and effective tool to protect the interest of all parties: the
technology owner and their end users.  

In the presentation of Maurice the following subjects will be covered: 

*	What is escrow? 
*	Different kinds of escrow? 
*	User's standpoint? 
*	Supplier's standpoint? 
*	What is a balanced escrow agreement? 
*	How to verify the deposit (different levels of verification)? 
*	What about documentation? 
*	What about updates? 
*	How to use the deposit when it is released?

We hope to see you on this very interesting security event!
