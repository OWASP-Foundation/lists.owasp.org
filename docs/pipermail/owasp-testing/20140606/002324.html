So um, catastrophic is a pretty high bar[1].  Some other events that could
be considered catastrophic:
* openssl
* actual bad things happening other than bruised egos

Now I know that our industry has a reputation for high-drama egos, but why
not consider, for just a moment, that this was done as a good faith
exercise to help ensure that all flagship-labelled projects are *actually*
flagship quality?

Rather than spending your time fretting about what the process will be to
get the flagship status back once the processes are sorted out, why not
focus on making your project continue to kick ass, especially after
repeated acknowledgement that it was, and should continue to be a flagship
project?

We all want OWASP to be a better organization and burning cycles over
project status and labels is not helping make things better.

Cheers,
Yvan
