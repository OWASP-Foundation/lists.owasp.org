There are many ways to represent the diagrams. I suggest we stick to UML
sequence diagrams, state diagrams, activity diagrams and use cases as they
are well known and understood. 

How many people are away of Tufte's work? In particular, Envisioning
Information and the equally brilliant "Visual Explanations: Images and
Quantities, Evidence and Narrative"? In fact, all of his three major works
are worth the read and are beautiful objets d'art in their own right.

http://www.edwardtufte.com

See the image (which is one diagram from Envisioning Information) for an
"out of the box" idea.
