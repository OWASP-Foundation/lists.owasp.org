My $0.02.

Language purity shouldn't matter, as long as it's spelt correctly and  
the grammar is reasonably tight.

Australian English != UK English != US English != Canadian English.  
There is significant divergence between formal written English and  
spoken English wrt to "that" and "which" and other common usage. For  
example, Australian English has always been hard core about the "- 
yse" endings for more than a hundred years, but UK has only become  
mostly hard core since the anti-US sentiment in the last 50 years.  
Both have retained the old style "-our" endings except for fortnight,  
which the Americans don't commonly use. There are small things I  
guarantee you will miss if you're trying to make a "pure" UK English  
document. US English has infiltrated the planet, and we all use US  
English unconsciously now.

Good luck in your language purity efforts. I'd prefer we stick to  
creating and tech reviewing excellent quality material until final  
proof edit than waste time on nonsense like language purity. That's  
why the Guide is in US English - less to worry about from a  
localization point of view.

thanks,
Andrew
