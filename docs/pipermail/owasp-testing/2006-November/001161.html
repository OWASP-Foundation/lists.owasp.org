On 11/6/06, Alberto Revelli <a.revelli at reply.it> wrote:
> 2 doubts about the proposed definition:
>
> 1. The fact that is unexpected is not sufficient (in the mathematical
> meaning of the word) for it to be a vuln (an unexpected behavior could
> be a wrong rendering of screen colors, which is hardly a vuln). A more
> precise definition to me would be "<snip> unexpected behavior of either
> application or environment that represents a danger <snip>"
>
> 2. A vulnerability could be originated by a behavior that is 100%
> expected, but that has not been perceived as a vulnerability yet (e.g.:
> Cross Site Tracing attacks exploit what is precisely the expected
> behavior of the TRACE HTTP method)


Very, very, VERY well put...

How about this:

A Vulnerability is defined as A) an unexpected behavior of either
application or environment  that represents a danger or B) an expected
behavior but that has not been perceived as risk.
This behavior normally represent a danger of  CIA triad
(Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) being compromised and/or
potential exploitation.
