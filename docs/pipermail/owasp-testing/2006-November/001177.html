When I am working on a pen-test engagement I think about my actions as
"attacking", even if I am wearing a white hat instead of a black one,
since "penetration testing" intrinsically means having an "attacking
stance" and performing aggressive actions that can be 100% categorized
as "attacking a target".

Therefore, I do not see any negative connotation in the word "attacker"
and I think the word can perfectly fit in the context of a security test.

Of course, the word must not be used in a "black hat" fashion. Going
back to Dan's example, I would have written "the attacker needs to
encode..." or "in order to perform this attack we need to encode...".

That said, it's fine for me to switch to "tester" if the majority feels
it's better for the Guide :)

A.
