            NEW YORK METRO CHAPTER
  INFORMATION SYSTEMS SECURITY ASSOCIATION (ISSA)
   
  REMINDER!
   
  WEDNESDAY, JANUARY 11th, 2006 AT 2 PM
  IS THE CHAPTER'S FIRST EDUCATIONAL SESSION OF THE NEW YEAR!
   
  VENUE: PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP, 300 Madison Avenue at East 42nd Street, NYC, 22nd Floor.  Refreshments courtesy of PricewaterhouseCoopers.
   
  REGISTRATION ON OUR WEBSITE AT WWW.NYMISSA.org (follow the registration instructions)
  Please register by JANUARY 9th, 2006.
   
  CHALLENGES FOR INFORMATION SECURITY MANAGEMENT IN THE REAL WORLD - 2 Stimulating Presentations!
   
  Session 1:   JOHN CURRAN, Vice President & Deputy General Counsel, STROZ FRIEDBERG, LLC      
   
  "COMPUTER EVIDENCE, CONTROL AND PRIVACY".  
   
  ABSTRACT*: Corporations are increasing their monitoring activities to remain vigilant and stay ahead of potential risk and adversity.  However, as they monitor the premises and personnel what should a CSO / CISO be cognizant of and what are the legal guidelines and ramifications associated with these practices.
   
  BIO*:  Mr. Curran is Vice President, Deputy General Counsel of Stroz Friedberg, LLC, a technical services and consulting firm specializing in computer forensics, electronic discovery, cyber crime and private investigations. Prior to joining the firm, John served as Deputy General Counsel for National Security Affairs at the Federal Bureau of Investigation.  In that role, he provided counsel to the FBI Director and other senior FBI executives regarding its counterterrorism, counterintelligence and counterespionage missions, especially concerning sophisticated electronic surveillance matters.  He also represented the FBI in dealings with the Department of Justice, the Intelligence Community and Congressional staff.  For more than 12 years, John served as an Assistant U.S. Attorney for the Eastern District of New York; most recently, as a National Security Coordinator and Senior Litigation Counsel.  In addition to his government service, John worked as an associate at Skadden, Arps,
 Slate, Meagher & Flom LLP and as a senior litigator in the international practice group of Reed Smith LLP, he worked on a variety of complex matters in federal and state courts, including employee termination litigation, theft of trade secrets, as well as white collar criminal matters.
   
  Session 2: BEHNAM DAYANIM, PAUL HASTINGS         
   
  "GETTING TOUGH ON SECURITY BREACHES" 
   
   ABSTRACT*: California has enacted the Information Privacy Act SB 1386 that requires companies and government agencies that store personal information on California residents to disclose any breach of security.  Are there any other similar laws and what other states are moving to pass similar legislature?  
   
  BIO*: Mr. Behnam Dayanim possesses extensive experience in advising the financial services industry with respect to issues relating to electronic commerce.  Major clients include Optimal Payments Inc., Harris Financial Corporation and the online broker/dealer Harrisdirect LLC (formerly DLJdirect and CSFBdirect), on whose behalf he was extremely active in pressing successfully for federal electronic signature legislation.  He also advises major insurers and other financial services companies on trade and e-commerce issues and has represented several e-health and other online providers in connection with the negotiation of contracts, privacy and other regulatory compliance, and related matters.
