Just wanted to inquire on this occasion - Are the following possible within
the current scope of this chapter -

http://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_Working_Session_-_OWASP_Intra_Governmental_Affairs
<http://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_Working_Session_-_OWASP_Intra_Governmental_Affairs>
http://www.owasp.org/index.php/Protecting_Federal_Government_from_Web_2.0_Application_Security_Risks

<http://www.owasp.org/index.php/Protecting_Federal_Government_from_Web_2.0_Application_Security_Risks>We
could definitely conduct similar meetings / conferences with the Government
officials often explaining the risks in the current set-up and processes.
Wanted to ask if there is any plan related to this from our OWASP chapter
perspective.

One more thing - if we think that this "lack of sufficient security
controls" stops at Government machinery in India, we are absolutely wrong.
Do you know how easy it is to hack hotel networks in India and view the
credit card info; how easy it is to perform social engineering attacks in
India; and how easily we fall traps to internet based worms / viruses. My
idea is just by security fencing the government based infrastructure would
not help. The security consciousness should come within in the people
related to IT industry in the entire country.

On this front - I am not sure if I am throwing darts; I have not seen such a
complete implementation across - But what we need is a complete portal which
caters to the questions people might put forth related to the security. The
idea is to create security awareness among people through a portal which
acts as a advisory to the current internet threat scenarios - localized to
our country. Tech big wiggy companies need to endorse the site and provide
their own recommendations for implementing security controls specific to
current threats (e.g. prevailing worms, viruses). A forum / helpline could
also be placed wherein people could approach for inquires related to the
security. Finally and more importantly the site needs to be marketed well to
take it to the masses.

Our country's IT infrastructure needs a major overhaul wrt the security.

regards
Phani
