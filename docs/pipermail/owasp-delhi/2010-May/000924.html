Computer scientists led by Hovav Shacham, a UC San Diego professor, hacked
an electronic voting machine and stole votes using a malicious programming
approach that had not been invented when the voting machine was designed.
The computer scientists employed "return-oriented programming" to force a
Sequoia AVC Advantage electronic voting machine to turn against itself and
steal votes

**On March 29, 2010, Pune Mirror carried a report on how Electronic Voting
Machines (EVMs) are easy to hack into....
