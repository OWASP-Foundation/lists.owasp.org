Well I'm no expert on XSS, but generally I'd prefer input sanitization.

Consider the following:
1 with multiple interfaces (outputs) its easier and more secure to clean it at the source before persistence.

2 Many times the interfaces or outputs change. Developers may forget security constraints over time and buld sexy UIs that disregard earlier sanitization code.

3. Unsanitized data sitting in your DB can be atime bomb.

The points are correlated, but gives you a perspective why I'd rate input better but it depends on your scenario.

The only down of input sanitization is u gotta be careful how to do it; tougher than escaping XML on output. But theb again that's what OWASP AntiSamy tools are for :)
