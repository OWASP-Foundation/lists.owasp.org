There is surely a lot happening in the Government and what remains to be
seen is the official formation of a Cyber Security Authority which may be
under the Min of Defense or may report to the PMO directly. In any case,
there is a lot more at stake in the face of a "cyber attack" as we all
know.

The media will talk about defacement and we all are looking for breaches and
compromised servers / information.

While we may have secured the pathways to the PMO and the 450 + departments,
have we also done the same for critical infrastructure ? The railways,
electricity grid, nuclear facilities, e-governance network .... all this
stuff  ? Everything is "connected" and we know the state of security at most
places is not something to be proud of.

There is a lot we can say and do but I would like to make a point here -
before we think of raising our personal mohalla cyber armies and wage war,
or go to light candles to express our solidarity with the hacked departments
let us think about the work we do - how proud are you about the information
security implementation that has been undertaken in any major / minor
organization ? How confident you are that that organization will be able to
repel / contain / respond to a breach. Rather do you think your client will
be able to recognize an attack ! So first let us stop clients from asking
for substandard implementations (they work on dynamically shrinking budgets
and the consultants are forced to cut corners)

Once the buyer imbibes the culture of recognizing and demanding quality work
half the battle will be won at home itself !

Dinesh
