Agree with the general thoughts that are being discussed. My fear is, with
all the emotional response pouring in, we might loose sight of the real
issue here and more importantly this might all fizz out in a while like so
many other incidents in the past.

Those who died on 26/11 in Mumbai and during all the previous terrorist
attacks are not just the victims of a bullet or a blast. They are a victim
of a system, plagued with inept people who are in power and with countrymen
who will do the talk but not walk the walk. It is a shame. To a certain
degree, we are all to blame as we are responsible for making our country
what it is today.

I agree with Druv, we as a country lack leaders who can inspire with words
and follow through with actions. Corruption is a part of the problem, not
the only problem.

Terrorism is a part of life in today's world. Given its nature, no country
can stop it but as pointed out, we need to be prepared for it. Here, in some
sense, philosophically, you can draw a parallel between terrorism and
application security (or insecurity).

We were unprepared, we are unprepared. We always knew it but were hoping
that we might be lucky and noone would notice us. 

We are a democracy where people are taken for granted and a part of the
problem is that we, the people, have always accepted it as how things will
always be. We fume for a , blame each other, at best "discuss ideas" and
then move on with our own indivisual busy lives.

I'm not going to mention everything that is wrong with our country but
instead make an appeal that we, the people, need to come together and act,
and act now. It is only the educated youth of this country, the common man
who can bring in the change that we all wish to see. Let us not get bogged
down by the system and the inept leaders we have. 

I and a few friends of mine are willing to volunteer and help in our limited
capacity if someone is aware of a "meaningful" movement or platform that is
out there. 

(Not sure if Dharmesh (OWASP Mumbai) hangs out here, but would be worthwhile
to float this in the Mumbai community as well)

It is time to act, lets start with a step.

thanks,
-Jitesh Kamat

3. Rich people want to rule world utilizing money.

4. Poor / Unemployed people need money for food and life and they become
prime target of such opportunities.

 

No one can remove corruption. But yes physical security can be improved to
have zero-effect of such attacks.

 

My ROOT suggestions for PHYSICAL SECURITY are:

1.      Install scanners on all public places instead of CCTV. These
scanners should automatically send the signal / mail to all local, state
level and central security agencies along with place and pictures.

2.      These scanners should be governed by Army police. 

3.      Defense Ministry should respond on these signal using different
mediums newspaper, websites etc.

4.      There should be one Security Index like BSE, NIFTY which will
present the exact value of security measures and effectiveness.

 

Media and other Intellectual society should present a plan to government on
how we can improve this.

 

Our scientists should demand fund for and develop such scanners.
