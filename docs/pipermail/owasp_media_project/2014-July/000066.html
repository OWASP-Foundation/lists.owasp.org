Hi Ian,

thanks for your comments it's greatly appreciated.

I haven't have the time to do a "report" on what we did at last AppSecEU
but we had pretty good camera hardware (Panasonic AGHMC41; Panasonic
AGHMC151).

If you want to backtrack what we've done there's some email post in the
archive for this, notably:
http://lists.owasp.org/pipermail/owasp_media_project/2013-December/000033.html
http://lists.owasp.org/pipermail/owasp_media_project/2014-May/000051.html

That said, I'm agreeing with you on the quality parts, but we have
limitations we should consider (and you named some):
- Every setup for every conference will be different as we change venue
each year in OWASP (in other conference they often stay at the same place)
- Quality of audio will ALWAYS depend on the room and local staff except
if we have a dedicated wireless mic for streaming and that can be more
problematic
- Everyone will have their view on what a stream should be.
- Costs and budget. We will never have a big budget for streaming,
however we can have a decent one.

For the capture part, I'm fairly sure that "tapping" the video stream is
one good option. We have bought 3 X-CAPTURE-1
http://solarisjapan.com/products/xcapture-1-usb-3-0-hd-capture-unit to
stream the cameras at AppSecEU and it worked fine. Only limitation is
that it is Windows (DirectX) compatible only but they capture the
greatest range of outputs on earth for that price (around 350$USD).

The only problem I have with this is the position of the laptop that is
connected to the capture card. At AppSecEU, the filming was done from
the back of the room.

I'm really a prototype trying kind of guy and you can see the different
output we got:

direct capture of the screen with google hangout + Zoom H1
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpr-xdpM8wG80aEXBZV9lk5nkEf3q-jaz

proprietary software with provided laptops and post audio mixing from
room pro audio (worked pretty badly as we missed many talks)
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpr-xdpM8wG8ODR2zWs06JkMmlRiLyBXU

open broadcaster software + x-capture-1 hd input + panasonic pro camera
+ xlr input from room pro audio
https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpr-xdpM8wG_KHsxepT9o6trkqDELhr3_

At the point we are now, I think the solution is to use the X-CAPTURE-1
to have a "tap" on VGA and RCA audio from the room audio. Then, we use a
webcam for the presenter.

The reason I'm going towards that is that it is the most cost efficient
setup and we don't need the presenter face in HD. People are here for
the slides and demo outputs. I also want a setup that works for live
event or recording. So doing offline post is out of option. The webcam
is just because it will serve as a capture device over the X-CAPTURE-1.

I'll be beta testing this in OWASP Montreal's talks (often in French):
https://www.youtube.com/user/owaspmontreal/videos


If you are motivated to contribute I think we could use someone like you
who seems to have experience with all this and most importantly wants to
have great content online.

Our roadmap is here
https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_Media_Project#tab=Road_Map_and_Getting_Involved
but I think that with time we will actually shape the way streaming is
made within all OWASP. In my opinion OWASP is not a big corporation and
neither just a local conference, so we could be one of the great source
of free video content regarding appsec on the net (I think we already
are, but could be better).

The next big thing is AppSecUSA in Denver, but they will manage
themselves their own recording and streaming options. I'll be
"consulting" them to make sure it is as best as we could but I just plan
managing the upload of content on YouTube after the conference (will be
easier that way). I'll try to make contents from the Project Summit but
this will be way easier as I will just use a hangout setup.

By the way, consider yourself "in" the OWASP Media Project already. If
we need someone in Denver I'll contact you since you are somewhat close.

Thanks for jumping in,

- Jonathan
