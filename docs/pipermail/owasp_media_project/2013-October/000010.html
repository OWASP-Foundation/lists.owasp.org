I'm not a lawyer so I can't tell.

One thing I know, is that everything that was previously aired somewhere
from people with copyright infringement system are automatically counted
as infringement on YouTube. The scan is made just after you upload.

Even if we are legally right, YouTube won't care and will make a strike
anyways. And it's almost impossible or too much expensive (in time) to
contest.

We cannot even have a content we own with a song that is playing in the
background that is copyrighted.

If you want to make it up, we will need to see what contact we have with
YouTube in order to get /owasp. I fear that our "strike" will be in the
system for 6 months if we don't use any Google contacts.

If we don't have anyone, the plan will be to wait 6 months and get 30k+
views in the last 3 months of that waiting time (or maybe after) in
order to get some recognition from YouTube.

In the mean time, you can complete (as OWASP?) this
https://www.youtube.com/copyright_school

I don't think disputing the strike will help, mostly because doing so
will be like a big process and we will lose precious time doing it.

- Jonathan
