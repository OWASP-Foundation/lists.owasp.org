I think it all comes done to how exploitable this could be in the future.  I remember the old days
when we rated XSS as low because it required user action (click through or open up a web page). 
However, giving the newer attack vectors and motivations, we started to give it higher rating. 

The primary attack vector for Regex violation is most likely XSS.  From an attackers perspective,
if it takes more than two steps to exploit the vulnerability, i.e. click through or launch the
page to the malicious site with more than two clicks, it becomes painful to construct a viable
exploit.  

Until a viable exploit comes out, I am incline to give it a low rating.  Of course, things change
in our field pretty quickly, so we shall see.

> > uses java.sql.PreparedStatements or rdbms StoredProcedures, the data
> > is passed as precompiled binary or parametric data before it reaches

> > these types of data and command injections.
> >
