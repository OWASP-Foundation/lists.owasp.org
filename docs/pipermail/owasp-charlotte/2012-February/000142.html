> All,
> 
> After thinking about the nomination/selection of seven chapter board
> members last night (about half of the meeting's attendees), I suggest
> that we reduce the number of board members to a reasonable amount
> given the relatively small size of our membership. We should define
> some board member roles and responsibilities that will best suit our
> chapter, and select one board member best suited for each role. As the
> chapter's membership and involvement grows, we can add more board
> members to these or other roles not yet defined. For now, we should
> select from chapter members who feel that they meet one the role
> requirements. John Melton and I have come up with the following roles
> that we feel would most benefit the chapter.
> 
> Roles
> 
> Chapter Leader - Jon Molesa. Everybody's happy with this choice and
> we're confident that Jon will do a fine job.
> 
> Treasurer - This dedicated treasurer would manage the chapter's
> finances. The treasurer must be trustworthy (loaded statement) and
> should have no professional relationships with any other board members
> to avoid conflict of interest. The treasurer does not need security
> expertise or industry contacts. The treasurer must be able to manage
> budgets and spend wisely.
> 
> University Representative - This person should represent the
> university and act as a local liaison between the chapter's
> professionals and students, as well as between the chapter and the
> university. The OWASP Charlotte chapter has a strong student
> contingent and we've greatly benefitted from a relationship with the
> UNCC College of Computing and Informatics. This board seat is vital to
> the chapter. I believe that Dr. Chu is the only logical choice for
> this seat.
> 
> Professional Representative - This person should have solid expertise
> and contacts within the Information Security industry. This person
> should also have experience running or participating in OWASP chapters
> or functions. This person will serve in the interest of OWASP
> Charlotte. It is critical that OWASP Charlotte be free of corporate
> control or influence.
> 
> Possible Role
> 
> Junior/Student Board Member - This person would be a UNCC student and
> represent peer students, organize student events, and work with other
> board members to provide chapter mentorship or other opportunities for
> students.
> 
> Anybody interested in being considered for these roles should submit
> their names. We can make the final board selection the first order of
> business during our next meeting, or we can vote via email, doesn't
> matter.
> 
> Finally, I'd like to remove my name from consideration for these board
> member roles. I have no interest in being the treasurer, I'm not
> affiliated with the university, and I simply don't have the industry
> contacts that will benefit the chapter. I have strong technological
> and information security expertise that I will contribute to the
> chapter as a standard member.
>
