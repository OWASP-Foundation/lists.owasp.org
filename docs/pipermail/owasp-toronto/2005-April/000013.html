As a former employee of a Government department that still has a
vulnerable website, I feel it prudent to start any newly formed group
will a fundamental discussion on Secure Coding 101. Some possible topics
include:

Cross-Site Scripting
SQL Injection
Cookies
Sessions
Forms & User Input
Etc ...

Invariably public meetings like this will see some folks that could use
at least a refresher before delving deeper. Can't wait to see some
meetings starting!

Hi,

 I'd personally like to see 

1 - the technical detail and demos that of the various tools and
exploits.  I spend time discussing these things with developers and
product managers and showing samples of what I have been able to do with
their sites - I'd like to formalize that more.  More WebGoat/Scarab
tools

2 - comparison of the various frameworks for security - ISO17799 vs
CoBIT vs Privacy law etc.

3 - comparison/discussion of OSSTM and other methodologies and successes
with it and others

4 - might be good to start a newletter to provide a forum for chapter
members to provide articles on topics of interest - perhaps not for the
first meeting...

5 - Discussion of recent patch and virus experiences and successes
around them.  Did a member have a particularly effective tool/process in
place to combat the problem and can share it?

That's a start -- looking forward to the first meeting...
