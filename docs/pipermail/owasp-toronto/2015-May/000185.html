Hi everyone,


This year, SecTor -- Security Education Conference Toronto, has officially
partnered with the OWASP Toronto chapter and the OWASP Foundation to ensure
that as many members as possible can experience and benefit from Canada’s
largest IT Security Conference.  Use discount code *OWASP2015* to receive
10% off the current full conference registration price, or attend the Expo
only for free with discount code *OWASP2015expo.  *In addition, *groups of
5 or more* registering together are eligible for *20%* off by emailing Renu
at renu at sector.ca.



Now in its 9th year, the annual conference is Canada’s premier IT Security
event. SecTor brings together experts from around the world to share their
latest research and techniques involving underground threats and corporate
defence. The event has grown year over year with the support of Industry
Partners, like OWASP, whose members reap the benefits of a Canadian IT
security education event.  Held at the Metro Toronto Convention Centre in
downtown Toronto, SecTor 2015 runs two full days, October 20th and 21st,
with an optional training day on October 19th. The conference features over
50 Sessions and Keynotes from the industry’s most respected and trusted
experts.



Additional benefits include:


   - Opportunity to earn up to 24 CPE Credits
   - A learning track for every level of expertise: Technical, Management,
   Sponsor and Security Fundamentals
   - The Expo Theatre - where you can meet and learn from our sponsors
   - SecTor coffee lounge where you can plug-in and recharge throughout the
   day
   - Reception, Dinner and Party @ Steam Whistle Brewery - for all Full
   Conference Attendees
   - The Lock-Pick Village (a SecTor tradition) returns, and new activities
   are coming for 2015



SecTor speakers are true security professionals with depth of understanding
on topics that matter, making SecTor a must attend event for every IT
Professional.  Check out our website at www.SecTor.ca.  Join in the
conversation on LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, or at our blog, all accessible
at www.SecTor.ca/blog.
