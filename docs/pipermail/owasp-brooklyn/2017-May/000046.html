Good morning everyone,



The New York State Office of Information Technology Services, the
University at Albany, State University of New York, and The NYS Forum,
Inc., are pleased to invite you to the *20th Annual New York State Cyber
Security Conference *and* 12th Annual Symposium on Information Assurance.  *The
event takes place June 7 and 8 at the Empire State Plaza in Albany, N.Y.,
with a pre-conference training opportunity on June 6.



The annual event offers an opportunity to learn more about today’s cyber
challenges facing government and businesses; what you can do to protect
your workplace from cyber threats; and how to increase your organization’s
cyber resiliency*.*



New York State is a recognized leader in the industry and Governor Andrew
M. Cuomo remains committed to ensuring New York’s digital economy can
continue to safely grow.  By bringing government and the private sector
together for this two-day event, we are strengthening the resiliency and
security of our State.



The 2017 conference offers something for everyone – whether you’re a user
of technology, IT professional, government employee, business manager or
educator.  The conference will feature more than 50 dynamic breakout
sessions, led by industry experts, covering topics ranging from risk
management, mobile security, the threat landscape, the human element,
incident response and more.



*Register today at* www.its.ny.gov/eiso/conference/2017.



Cost to Attend:

   - *Free* to government and public sector employees
   - $50 for not-for-profit employees*
   - $200 for private sector employees*

*Group discount of 20% for 5 or more employees from the same organization.



For more information on the conference agenda, visit the conference website
at www.its.ny.gov/eiso/conference/2017.



I look forward to seeing you at the 20th Annual New York State Cyber
Security Conference and 12th Annual Symposium on Information Assurance.
