Dear OWASP fellows,

We invite you to our next OWASP event: the BeNeLux OWASP Day 2016!

The OWASP BeNeLux Day 2016 will be held on March 17-18 in Esch-sur-Alzette
(Luxembourg), jointly organized by the OWASP chapters in Belgium, the
Netherlands and Luxembourg, hosted and co-organized by the University of
Luxembourg and the Interdisciplinary Centre for Security, Reliability and
Trust (SnT).

The OWASP BeNeLux Day 2016 offers free 1-day trainings and a free 1-day
conference.
The event will take place on 17 and 18 March 2016, in Belval Campus, in
Esch-sur-Alzette - Luxembourg.

On Thursday 17 March, four free 1-day trainings are offered:

   - OWASP SSL advanced forensic tool (by Achim Hoffman)
   - Application Security Primer (by Martin Knobbloch)
   - Hands-on Threat Modeling (by Sebastien Deleersnyder)
   - OWASP Security Shepherd (by Mark Denihan)

On Friday 18 March, the 1-day conference features a line-up of various
regional and international speakers:

   - *Gamers, You're the new Botnets* (Julie Gommes)
   - *OWASP Top 10 Privacy Risks* (Stefan Burgmair)
   - *LangSec meets State Machines* (Erik Poll)
   - *The Tales of a Bug Bounty Hunter *(Arne Swinnen)
   - *OWASP Secure Knowledge Framework* (Glenn & Riccardo Ten Cate)
   - *Serial Killer: Silently Pwning your Java Endpoints* (Christian
   Schneider & Alvaro Muñoz)
   - *Experiences with Paste-Monitoring* (Michael Hamm)

Attendance to the trainings and the conference are free of charge, but
registration is required. We have a limited number of seats available, so
first come first serve.

More information on the program and venue can be found at
http://www.owaspbenelux.eu

See you in Esch-Belval in March!
