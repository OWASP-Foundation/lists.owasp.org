Four and a half years later, virtualization is almost a mainstream technology. Surely it seemed that security would be a top issue in architecture, design and technology choice. 

As with any new technology there are new security issues to contend with. Entire network infrastructures now exist inside the virtualization layer, connecting servers.   

Sometimes we don't really see what our eyes are viewing. That's true with your computer screen, and it's true in nature as well.   

Businesses small and large are increasingly turning to virtualization technology to save costs and increase redundancies in case of disaster. 

Let us explore security challenges around virtualization by knowing the facts behind virtualization: 

•Virtualization may bypass secure configuration Standards 

•Security network scans may never detect insecure virtual servers 

•Information security must dig deeper to document virtualized IT environments for compliance requirements 

•Information security relied upon the slow rate of physical server deployment to “stay secure” 

•What could be high risks due to virtualized environments
