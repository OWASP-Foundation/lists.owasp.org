That's sort of right, the EFF formed and it was supposed to have various chapters. During that process it was decided not to have chapters so EFF Austin (EFF Texas) broke away. It's important that these two groups not be confused as they have a very divergent perspective and ethos with regard to where they draw the line on civil liberties. In general you'll find the local EFF Austin group is a bit more conservative and as a result tends to support legislation and activities that put a little more limit on personal freedom.

They busted Steve's company because Loyd Blankenship (The Mentor) was working there as the editor on the Cyberpunks books and the SS felt it was a handbook to hack. They also got Chris (Erik Bloodaxe). Part of the confusion was they thought that the SJG BBS was part of Loyd's Phoenix Project BBS (I was #19 Ravage).

Another group that formed in part because of this that was international and local was the Cypherpunks (http://www.wired.com/culture/lifestyle/news/1997/02/2082 ).
