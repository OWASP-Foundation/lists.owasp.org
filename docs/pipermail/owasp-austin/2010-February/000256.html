Austin Security Professionals Happy Hour
Austin OWASP and Capitol of Texas ISSA would like to cordially extend an 
invitation for you to attend our February Austin Security Professionals 
Happy Hour sponsored by WhiteHat Security.  The Happy Hour will be held on 
Thurday, February 11, 2010 from 5 PM to 7 PM at Sherlock's Baker Street 
Pub (9012 Research Blvd).  We will be in the party room located off to 
your right as you enter the main doors.  Don't miss out on this excellent 
opportunity for you to enjoy food and drinks while networking with fellow 
security professionals from Austin and the surround areas.

Advanced Persistent Threat - What Does it Mean for Applications Security? 
- Tuesday, February 23, 2010, 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM
For our February Austin OWASP meeting, we have Matt Pour, SE for Blue Coat 
Systems, and former Security Solutions Architect and X-Force Field 
Engineer for IBM ISS.  He will be talking to us about the Advanced 
Persisitent Threat and what it means for Application Security.  Targeted 
attacks, slow moving malware, foreign intelligence/government sponsored 
hackers, corporate/industrial espionage – all fun and games?  Not really. 
These vectors are occurring today, and the threat vector has bled into the 
application space.  What do you have to contend with once it passes 
through the firewall.  The meeting will be on Tuesday, February 23 from 
11:30 AM to 1:00 PM at National Instruments, Building C, as usual.  As an 
extra-special treat, this meeting will have lunch sponsored by Expanding 
Security, an awesome local company that you can leverage for great CISSP 
training, informative Security Summits, and much more!
